# ARIDONIA - MINERALS, RESOURCES & GEOLOGICAL TREASURES
## Complete Geological and Economic Resource Survey

---

## GEOLOGICAL OVERVIEW

Aridonia's geology spans 500+ million years, from ancient stable cratons to actively forming mountains. The continent hosts exceptional mineral diversity due to its complex tectonic history, creating both mundane and magical resource deposits.

---

## COMMON METALS & ORES

### **IRON (Fe)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary Deposits:** Ayarson Mountains (massive magnetite bodies)
- **Secondary Sites:** Kingdom of Rust foothills, northern Westallis
- **Formation:** Ancient sedimentary banded iron formations + volcanic deposits

#### **Types & Quality:**
- **Ayarson Magnetite:** Highest quality; 65-70% iron content
- **Rust Hematite:** Good quality; 55-65% iron content; reddish (kingdom named for this)
- **Bog Iron:** Low quality; 30-40% iron content; found in BitterMarsh wetlands

#### **Annual Production:** 500,000 tons (estimated)
- Ayarson: 300,000 tons (60%)
- Kingdom of Rust: 150,000 tons (30%)
- Others: 50,000 tons (10%)

#### **Economic Value:** Foundation of all economies; 1 SP per 10 kg raw ore
#### **Cultural Significance:** Dwarven identity tied to iron; "Ayar" = iron in Khazdul

#### **Mining Settlements:**
- **Iron-Delve** (Ayarson): Population 8,000; deepest iron mine (1,200m)
- **Rust-Ford** (Kingdom of Rust): Population 5,000; open-pit operations
- **Ferropolis** (Kingdom of Rust): Population 12,000; major smelting center

---

### **COPPER (Cu)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary:** Central mountains (porphyry copper deposits)
- **Secondary:** Cerulea (native copper in basalt)
- **Formation:** Volcanic-hydrothermal systems

#### **Types & Quality:**
- **Mountain Copper:** Chalcopyrite; 1-3% copper content
- **Native Copper:** Pure copper nuggets; rare but prized
- **Cerulea Green Copper:** Contains traces of magic; higher value

#### **Annual Production:** 50,000 tons
#### **Economic Value:** 5 SP per kg (standard); 10 SP per kg (magical)
#### **Uses:** Bronze-making, electrical/magical conductors, decorative

#### **Notable Deposits:**
- **Copper-Peak Mine** (Central Mountains): Largest producer
- **Azure-Veins** (Cerulea): Magical copper; kingdom exports

---

### **TIN (Sn)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary:** Coastal placer deposits (western archipelago)
- **Secondary:** Vein deposits in central mountains

#### **Annual Production:** 10,000 tons (limited; tin is scarce)
#### **Economic Value:** 10 SP per kg
#### **Strategic Importance:** CRITICAL - essential for bronze; wars fought over deposits
#### **Control:** Kingdom of Rust controls 60% of production

---

### **LEAD (Pb)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary:** Galena veins in central mountains
- **Associated:** Often found with silver (lead-silver ores)

#### **Annual Production:** 30,000 tons
#### **Uses:** Plumbing, roofing, anti-magic shielding, spell components
#### **Economic Value:** 2 SP per kg
#### **Health Note:** Dwarves know it's poisonous; strict handling protocols

---

### **ZINC (Zn)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary:** Sphalerite deposits in Ayarson
- **Formation:** Hydrothermal veins

#### **Annual Production:** 15,000 tons
#### **Uses:** Brass-making, corrosion resistance
#### **Economic Value:** 4 SP per kg
#### **Discovery:** Dwarves isolated it 200 years ago; technological advantage

## PRECIOUS METALS

### **GOLD (Au)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Placer Deposits:** Rivers throughout western regions (especially Debrica)
- **Lode Deposits:** Central mountains, particularly high elevations
- **Desert Deposits:** Rare nuggets in SasSert Desert (surface finds)

#### **Types & Occurrence:**
- **River Gold:** Nuggets and flakes in alluvial deposits
- **Vein Gold:** Quartz veins in mountain regions
- **Desert Gold:** Rare surface finds after erosion
- **Magical Gold:** Rare variant that holds enchantments better

#### **Annual Production:** 15 tons (approximate)
- Central Mountains: 8 tons
- Debrica Rivers: 5 tons
- Desert Finds: 2 tons

#### **Economic Value:** Standard currency base; 1 GP = 50 SP
#### **Cultural Significance:** 
- Vorthic Empire: Symbol of sun/fire; ceremonial use
- Dwarves: Appreciated but not obsessed with it
- Western Kingdoms: Status symbol; hoarding common

#### **Notable Sites:**
- **Gilded-Reach** (Central Mountains): Richest lode mine; heavily guarded
- **Gold-Wash Creek** (Debrica): Famous placer deposit; caused gold rush 50 years ago
- **The Glass Sea** (Vorth Empire): Occasionally yields nuggets on surface

---

### **SILVER (Ag)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary:** Central mountains (argentite, native silver)
- **Secondary:** Lead-silver ores (galena with silver content)
- **Magical:** Some veins in ley line intersections

#### **Annual Production:** 80 tons
#### **Economic Value:** 1 GP = 10 SP; higher for magical silver
#### **Uses:** Currency, jewelry, magic items, anti-undead weapons

#### **Special Types:**
- **Moonsilver:** Found only during full moons; glows faintly; 10x value
- **Pure Silver:** Native nuggets; 2x standard value
- **Spell Silver:** Near ley lines; holds enchantments; 5x value

#### **Notable Mines:**
- **Moonshaft Mine** (Central Mountains): Produces 30% of continent's silver
- **Silver-Falls** (Northern Westallis): Waterfall exposes silver veins

---

### **PLATINUM (Pt)**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Extremely Rare:** Only 3 known sites on continent
- **Primary:** Deep mines in Ayarson (3,000+ meters depth)
- **Placer:** Trace amounts in some river systems

#### **Annual Production:** <100 kg
#### **Economic Value:** 1 PP = 10 GP; extremely valuable
#### **Uses:** Highest-level magic items, divine artifacts, royal regalia
#### **Cultural Significance:** Dwarven kings wear platinum; no one else has access

#### **Formation:** Brought up from deep earth in ancient eruptions

- **Star Diamond:** Shows asterism; 10x normal value; very rare

### **MAGICAL GEMSTONES**

#### **MANASTONES**
- **Appearance:** Clear crystals that pulse with inner light
- **Distribution:** Only near ley line intersections
- **Formation:** Concentrated ambient magic crystallizes over centuries
- **Uses:** Store magical energy; power magical devices
- **Value:** 500-5,000 GP depending on storage capacity
- **Annual Discovery:** 20-50 stones
- **Cultural Impact:** Wars fought over major deposits

#### **SOULSTONES**
- **Appearance:** Deep purple crystals with swirling interior
- **Distribution:** Rare; found in ancient battlefields and execution sites
- **Formation:** Death magic concentrates into crystal form
- **Uses:** Necromancy, soul trapping (illegal in most kingdoms)
- **Value:** 1,000-10,000 GP (black market)
- **Legal Status:** Possession is crime in western kingdoms
- **Cultural Note:** Vorth Empire tolerates controlled use

#### **LIFESTONES**
- **Appearance:** Vibrant green crystals that feel warm
- **Distribution:** Ancient forests, particularly Sprout Kingdom
- **Formation:** Concentrated life force from old-growth forests
- **Uses:** Healing magic, resurrection components
- **Value:** 2,000-20,000 GP
- **Availability:** Extremely rare; Sylvanic druids control access
- **Production:** Cannot be synthesized; only found naturally

## FOSSIL FUELS & COMBUSTIBLES

### **COAL**

#### **Distribution:**
- **Primary:** Kingdom of Rust (massive deposits; kingdom named for oxidized surface coal)
- **Secondary:** Northern Westallis
- **Formation:** Ancient swamp forests compressed over 300 million years

#### **Types & Quality:**
- **Anthracite:** Highest quality; 90%+ carbon; rare; deep mines
- **Bituminous:** Standard coal; 60-80% carbon; most common
- **Lignite:** Low quality; 40-60% carbon; surface deposits

#### **Annual Production:** 5 million tons
- Kingdom of Rust: 4 million tons (80%)
- Others: 1 million tons (20%)

#### **Economic Value:** 1-5 SP per ton (depending on quality)
#### **Uses:** Heating, metalworking, industrial processes
#### **Cultural Impact:** Fueled Kingdom of Rust's industrial revolution
#### **Environmental Note:** Smoke creates health issues in mining towns

#### **Major Mining Centers:**
- **Coal-Town** (Kingdom of Rust): Population 15,000; largest coal producer
- **Black-Shaft** (Kingdom of Rust): Deep anthracite mine
- **Smoke-Stack** (Kingdom of Rust): Major processing center

## MAGICAL MATERIALS

### **MITHRIL**

#### **Appearance:** Silver-blue metal; lightweight but incredibly strong
#### **Distribution:** Extremely rare; deep mines in Ayarson only
#### **Formation:** Unknown; possibly magical transformation of silver
#### **Properties:**
- Weight: 1/3 of steel
- Strength: 2x steel strength
- Magic Conductivity: Excellent
- Corrosion Resistance: Absolute

#### **Annual Production:** <500 kg (entire continent)
#### **Economic Value:** 500-1,000 GP per kg
#### **Uses:** Highest quality weapons/armor, magical artifacts
#### **Cultural Significance:** Dwarven master smiths work exclusively with mithril
#### **Control:** Ayarson Clans monopoly; tightly controlled exports

#### **Known Deposits:**
- **Mithril-Deep** (Ayarson): Only producing mine; 4,000m deep
- **Lost Mithril Vein** (Ruins of Prauka): Legendary; possibly depleted

---

### **ADAMANTINE**

#### **Appearance:** Black metal with green sheen; impossibly hard
#### **Distribution:** Meteorite fragments only; no terrestrial deposits
#### **Formation:** Extraterrestrial; fallen from sky in ancient meteor strikes
#### **Properties:**
- Hardness: Can cut any material
- Durability: Virtually indestructible
- Magic Resistance: Extremely high
- Weight: Heavier than lead

#### **Annual Discovery:** 1-5 kg (from new meteorite finds)
#### **Economic Value:** 5,000-10,000 GP per kg
#### **Uses:** Legendary weapons, cutting edges for tools, magical research
#### **Cultural Significance:** Possession of adamantine weapon = legendary status
#### **Known Sources:** 
- Old meteorite impact sites (mostly depleted)
- Occasional new meteorites
- Ancient dwarven vaults (hoarded for millennia)

---

### **COLD IRON**

#### **Appearance:** Dull gray iron; never rusts
#### **Distribution:** Rare veins in Ayarson; must be worked without heat
#### **Formation:** Unknown; possibly ley line interaction with iron
#### **Properties:**
- Never corrodes
- Disrupts fey magic
- Harmful to fey creatures
- Cannot be heated without losing properties

#### **Annual Production:** 2,000 kg
#### **Economic Value:** 50-100 GP per kg
#### **Uses:** Anti-fey weapons, protection against fey magic
#### **Cultural Note:** Dwarves developed techniques to work it cold

---

## RESOURCE CONFLICTS & CONTROL

- Kingdom of Rust monopolized tin
- Western kingdoms formed coalition

- Peace treaty guarantees tin access

### **Current Tensions:**

**Mithril Monopoly:**
- Ayarson controls all production
- Other nations resentful
- Black market smuggling common

**Coal Dependency:**
- Most nations rely on Kingdom of Rust coal

**Magical Material Scarcity:**
- Increasing magic use depleting manastones

## GEOLOGICAL HAZARDS

### **Mining Dangers:**
- Cave-ins (common)
- Flooding (deep mines)
- Toxic gases (coal mines)

- Magical anomalies (mana-rich areas)

### **Prevention:**
- Dwarven safety protocols (best)

- Systematic support structures

---

## CONSERVATION & SUSTAINABILITY

### **Depleting Resources:**
- Surface tin deposits (nearly exhausted)
- Old-growth timber (over-harvested)
- Easily accessible minerals (requiring deeper mining)

### **Renewable Resources:**
- Timber (if replanted)
- Clay (weathering produces more)
- Salt (evaporation continues)

### **Non-Renewable:**
- All metals
- Coal
- Magical materials (form over millennia)

---

This geological survey represents current knowledge of Aridonia's mineral wealth. New deposits are occasionally discovered, and mining technology continues to advance, making previously inaccessible deposits viable. The relationship between resource control and political power remains the dominant factor in continental geopolitics.
