## GLOBAL CLIMATE PATTERNS ANALYSIS

### OCEAN CURRENTS - CRITICAL FACTOR

**Your map doesn't explicitly show ocean currents, but they are CRUCIAL for realism.**

#### Recommended Current System for Realism:

**1. Central Ocean Gyre (Subtropical)**
- **Direction:** Clockwise circulation (Northern Hemisphere convention)
- **Western Boundary Current:** Warm current flowing north along eastern Aridonia/Orynt
  - **Effect:** Warms eastern coasts, increases precipitation
  - **Makes sense with:** Forests on eastern Aridonia coast
- **Eastern Boundary Current:** Cool current flowing south along western Boreas/Austra
  - **Effect:** Cools western coasts, may reduce precipitation
  - **Could explain:** If western coasts are less lush than expected

**2. Northern Ocean Gyre (Subpolar)**
- **Direction:** Counterclockwise
- **Effect:** Brings cold water south, warm water north
- **Impact on The Girdle:** Moderate temperatures despite high latitude

**3. Equatorial Currents (if equator is south of map)**
- Would flow west to east
- Would warm southern regions

**4. Southern Circumpolar Current**
- Flows west to east around the planet
- Cools southern regions
- Creates stormy conditions

#### How This Affects Your Biomes:

**IF you have a cold current along southern/eastern Aridonia:**
- Coastal desert at Vorth Harbour = REALISTIC ✓
- Example: Atacama Desert with Humboldt Current

**IF you have a warm current along eastern Aridonia:**
- Should have MORE vegetation near the coast
- Example: Eastern Australia, southeastern United States

**Recommendation:** DEFINE YOUR OCEAN CURRENTS - they're the key to realistic climate
