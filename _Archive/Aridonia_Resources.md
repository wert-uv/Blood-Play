## METALLIC ORES & PRECIOUS METALS

### CENTRAL MOUNTAIN RANGE (Dwarven City of Ayarson & Vorth Loge Region)

**IRON ORE**
- **Primary Deposits:** Magnetite and hematite veins in igneous intrusions
- **Location:** Deep mines around Ayarson, extending 500+ meters underground
- **Quality:** High-grade ore (60-70% iron content)
- **Annual Production:** Sufficient to supply regional needs
- **Processing:** Smelted in charcoal furnaces, forges throughout mountain settlements
- **Uses:** Weapons, tools, construction materials, trade commodity
- **Strategic Value:** CRITICAL - Basis of military power
- **Dwarven Control:** Ayarson maintains near-monopoly on highest-quality iron

**COPPER**
- **Deposits:** Porphyry copper deposits, malachite surface outcrops
- **Location:** Western slopes of central mountains, some eastern desert mountains
- **Mining:** Both deep shaft and open-pit where oxidized near surface
- **Uses:** Bronze (with tin), coinage, cooking vessels, roofing, piping
- **Trade:** Major export from mountain kingdoms
- **Cultural:** Green patina valued aesthetically

**TIN**
- **Deposits:** Cassiterite in stream placers and hard rock veins
- **Rarity:** Less common than copper, sought after
- **Location:** Northern mountain streams, some desert ranges
- **Importance:** Essential for bronze production
- **Trade:** Valuable commodity, sometimes traded from beyond Aridonia
- **Control:** Access to tin sources provides economic power

**SILVER**
- **Deposits:** Often associated with lead ores (galena)
- **Location:** Vorth Loge area, some Ayarson mines
- **Extraction:** Cupellation process separates silver from lead
- **Purity:** Mountain silver known for high purity
- **Uses:** Coinage (primary), jewelry, ceremonial objects, mirror backing
- **Value:** HIGH - Forms basis of currency
- **Cultural:** Associated with moon, magic, purity

**GOLD**
- **Placer Gold:** Found in mountain streams, particularly after spring floods
- **Panning:** Common supplemental activity for mountain villagers
- **Lode Gold:** Quartz veins in mountains, requires mining
- **Location:** Scattered throughout central mountains, some desert ranges
- **Purity:** Mountain gold averages 18-22 karat
- **Uses:** Coinage (highest value), jewelry, religious objects, gilding
- **Value:** EXTREME - Defines wealth
- **Trade:** Primary export for acquiring foreign goods
- **Cultural:** Associated with sun, divine power, royalty

**LEAD**
- **Source:** Galena (lead sulfide) often with silver
- **Uses:** Weights, seals, roofing, glazing
- **Processing:** Smelted at lower temperatures than iron
- **Toxicity:** Known to be harmful but widely used
- **Availability:** Common in mountain regions

**ZINC** (discovered recently, not widely used yet)
- **Occurrence:** Sphalerite deposits
- **Potential:** Brass production (copper + zinc), medicinal
- **Status:** Experimental, gradually being adopted

## PRECIOUS & SEMI-PRECIOUS STONES

### MOUNTAIN GEMSTONES

**SAPPHIRE**
- **Location:** Alpine metamorphic zones, alluvial deposits
- **Colors:** Blue (most prized), also yellow, pink, green
- **Formation:** High-temperature metamorphism of aluminum-rich rocks
- **Mining:** Both hard rock and placer mining
- **Quality:** Vorth Loge region famous for clear, deep blue specimens
- **Uses:** Royal jewelry, magical focuses, optical devices
- **Value:** EXTREME - Largest clear stones worth fortunes
- **Cultural:** Associated with truth, nobility, divine favor
- **Grading:** Clarity, color depth, size determine value

**RUBY** (Rare)
- **Occurrence:** Similar to sapphire (corundum), much rarer
- **Color:** Deep red (chromium content)
- **Location:** Few known deposits, closely guarded
- **Value:** Most valuable gemstone by weight
- **Uses:** Royal crowns, powerful magical items
- **Cultural:** Symbol of power, blood, life force
- **Monopoly:** Discovery of new deposit changes regional power

**EMERALD** (Very Rare)
- **Occurrence:** Found in very specific geological conditions
- **Location:** Rumored deposits in remote canyons
- **Color:** Vivid green (chromium and vanadium)
- **Inclusions:** "Garden" inclusions common, accepted
- **Value:** EXTREME for clear specimens
- **Cultural:** Associated with growth, healing, nature magic

**AMETHYST**
- **Location:** Geodes and veins in volcanic rocks
- **Color:** Purple (iron impurities in quartz)
- **Abundance:** More common than sapphire/ruby
- **Crystals:** Large, well-formed crystals possible
- **Uses:** Jewelry, decorative objects, religious items
- **Value:** Moderate (affordable luxury)
- **Cultural:** Associated with sobriety, clarity of mind, priesthood

**CITRINE**
- **Formation:** Heat-treated amethyst or natural
- **Color:** Yellow to orange
- **Abundance:** Fairly common
- **Uses:** Jewelry, decorative arts
- **Value:** Low to moderate
- **Confusion:** Sometimes passed off as topaz

**ROCK CRYSTAL (Clear Quartz)**
- **Abundance:** Common in mountains
- **Quality:** Best specimens very clear
- **Size:** Can form huge crystals
- **Uses:** Scrying stones, lenses, decorative, magical focuses
- **Properties:** Believed to store and focus energy
- **Cultural:** Important in magic and divination

**GARNET**
- **Colors:** Red (most common), also orange, green, purple
- **Occurrence:** Metamorphic rocks throughout mountains
- **Abundance:** Common
- **Uses:** Jewelry (lower value), abrasives
- **Industrial:** Hardness makes good for grinding/sanding
- **Cultural:** Protection stone, warrior's gem

**TOURMALINE**
- **Colors:** Every color (black, pink, green, blue, watermelon)
- **Crystal Form:** Distinctive triangular cross-section
- **Property:** Pyroelectric (generates charge when heated)
- **Uses:** Jewelry, believed to have protective powers
- **Rarity:** Depends on color (pink/red most valued)

**TOPAZ**
- **Colors:** Colorless, yellow, blue, pink
- **Location:** Pegmatites in granite regions
- **Hardness:** Very hard (8 on Mohs scale)
- **Uses:** Jewelry, cutting tools
- **Value:** Moderate to high depending on color
- **Imperial Topaz:** Golden yellow-orange, most valuable

## STONE & BUILDING MATERIALS

### GRANITE
- **Location:** Core of mountain ranges, exposed in many areas
- **Colors:** Pink, gray, black, white (speckled)
- **Properties:** Extremely hard, durable, weather-resistant
- **Uses:** Foundations, monuments, fortifications, millstones
- **Quality:** Mountain granite among best (coarse-grained, strong)
- **Quarrying:** Major industry, exported to lowlands
- **Famous:** Ayarson black granite prized for monuments

### MARBLE
- **Formation:** Metamorphosed limestone in mountain zones
- **Location:** Specific mountain valleys, limited occurrence
- **Colors:** White (purest), gray, pink, green veined
- **Properties:** Carves beautifully, polishes to shine
- **Uses:** Sculpture, decorative architecture, floors, counters
- **Value:** High-quality white marble very valuable
- **Export:** Major luxury trade item
- **Cultural:** Associated with wealth, refinement, permanence

### LIMESTONE
- **Occurrence:** Sedimentary formations, common in foothills
- **Uses:** Building stone, lime production (mortar, plaster)
- **Processing:** Burned in kilns to produce quicklime
- **Availability:** Abundant, forms whole formations
- **Quality Varies:** From soft chalk to hard limestone
- **Fossils:** Often contains fossils (sometimes collected)
- **Agriculture:** Ground for soil amendment (reduces acidity)

### SANDSTONE
- **Location:** Desert regions, some mountain formations
- **Colors:** Red, yellow, tan, brown (iron content)
- **Properties:** Easy to carve when fresh, hardens with exposure
- **Uses:** Building blocks, decorative carving, grinding stones
- **Desert Architecture:** Primary building material in Vorth Empire
- **Varieties:** From soft to hard depending on cement
- **Erosion:** Creates spectacular formations in desert

### SLATE
- **Formation:** Metamorphosed shale, splits into flat sheets
- **Location:** Mountain regions with shale parent rock
- **Colors:** Gray, black, purple, green
- **Uses:** Roofing tiles, flooring, writing tablets, chalkboards
- **Quality:** Best slate splits perfectly smooth
- **Export:** Roofing slate major trade commodity
- **Durability:** Can last centuries as roofing

### OBSIDIAN (Volcanic Glass)
- **Location:** Ancient volcanic areas (limited)
- **Properties:** Fractures to razor-sharp edges
- **Uses:** Blades, arrowheads, surgical tools, mirrors
- **Quality:** Black obsidian most common, "rainbow" rare
- **Cultural:** Sacred stone, used in ritual objects
- **Trade:** Valuable for regions without access
- **Magic:** Believed to have special properties (scrying, protection)

## CLAYS & CERAMICS

### POTTERY CLAY
- **Location:** River valleys, floodplains, wetlands
- **Quality:** Varies from coarse to fine
- **Processing:** Cleaned, aged, wedged before use
- **Uses:** Storage vessels, cooking pots, tiles, pipes
- **Firing:** Temperatures 800-1200°C depending on type
- **Regional Styles:** Each region has distinctive pottery
- **Economic:** Essential industry, everywhere produced

### FINE CLAY (Kaolin)
- **Location:** Weathered granite regions (limited)
- **Color:** Pure white
- **Uses:** Porcelain, fine ceramics, paper coating
- **Value:** Higher than common clay
- **Export:** Sought after for luxury goods
- **Properties:** Fires to white, holds fine detail

### BRICK CLAY
- **Abundance:** Common near rivers and floodplains
- **Uses:** Building bricks, roof tiles
- **Production:** Major industry near cities
- **Standardization:** Brick sizes vary by region
- **Quality:** Color and strength depend on firing

## FUEL SOURCES

### COAL
- **Location:** Sedimentary basins in foothills, some desert regions
- **Types:** Lignite (brown coal) to anthracite (hard coal)
- **Quality:** Mountain anthracite highest quality (hottest burning)
- **Mining:** Both surface and deep mining
- **Uses:** Smelting, smithing, heating, steam power (if developed)
- **Strategic Value:** CRITICAL for metalworking
- **Transport:** Heavy, expensive to transport far
- **Control:** Coal regions economically powerful

### CHARCOAL
- **Production:** Slow-burning wood in low oxygen (charcoal mounds)
- **Wood Sources:** Hardwoods (oak, beech) make best charcoal
- **Uses:** Smithing, cooking, heating
- **Industry:** Major forest industry
- **Sustainability:** Can deplete forests if not managed
- **Quality:** Burns hotter and cleaner than wood
- **Carbon:** Used in steel production (early forms)

### PEAT
- **Location:** Wetlands, particularly BitterMarsh region
- **Formation:** Partially decomposed plant matter
- **Harvesting:** Cut into blocks, dried
- **Uses:** Fuel (lower quality than wood), soil amendment
- **Availability:** Abundant in marsh regions
- **Cultural:** Traditional fuel for marsh peoples
- **Smoke:** Distinctive smoky smell when burned

### TIMBER (Fuel & Construction)
- **Quality Wood:** Oak, beech, ash, pine, cedar
- **Uses:** Construction, shipbuilding, fuel, charcoal
- **Management:** Forest laws protect valuable timber
- **Old Growth:** Ancient forests sacred/protected in some regions
- **Trade:** Major export from forested kingdoms
- **Types:** Hardwoods vs softwoods for different uses

## NATURAL OILS & RESINS

### PINE RESIN (Pitch)
- **Source:** Coniferous trees, especially pines
- **Collection:** Trees "tapped" for resin
- **Processing:** Distilled into turpentine and rosin
- **Uses:** Waterproofing (boats, barrels), adhesive, lamp fuel, medicine
- **Naval Stores:** Critical for shipbuilding
- **Incense:** Used in religious ceremonies

### LINSEED OIL
- **Source:** Pressed from flax seeds
- **Uses:** Wood finishing, paint medium, lamp fuel
- **Processing:** Cold-pressed (food) or heated (industrial)
- **Preservation:** Protects wood from moisture
- **Cultural:** Important in artistic traditions

### BEESWAX
- **Source:** Honeybee hives
- **Harvesting:** From wild or domestic hives
- **Uses:** Candles (best), waterproofing, polish, cosmetics
- **Value:** More expensive than tallow
- **Quality:** Burns clean, sweet smell
- **Cultural:** Church candles, luxury item

### TALLOW
- **Source:** Rendered animal fat (cattle, sheep)
- **Uses:** Candles (common), soap, cooking
- **Quality:** Inferior to beeswax (smokes, smells)
- **Availability:** Common, byproduct of butchering
- **Economy:** Major use for animal fat

## MINERAL SALTS

### ROCK SALT
- **Location:** Desert salt flats, ancient seabed deposits
- **Mining:** Both surface collection and mining
- **Purity:** Varies, pink/gray common (minerals), white prized
- **Uses:** Food preservation, flavoring, tanning, trade currency
- **Value:** EXTREME in regions far from sources
- **Trade:** Major desert export ("white gold")
- **Routes:** Salt caravans cross deserts
- **Cultural:** Symbolic of hospitality, preservation

### SEA SALT
- **Production:** Evaporation of seawater in coastal pans
- **Location:** Coastal regions, particularly southern
- **Quality:** Fine-grained, pure
- **Seasonal:** Production in dry season
- **Uses:** Same as rock salt, slightly different flavor
- **Industry:** Specialized coastal communities

### SALTPETER (Potassium Nitrate)
- **Occurrence:** Cave deposits, animal manure heaps
- **Uses:** Gunpowder (if discovered), preservative, fertilizer
- **Mining:** From caves, bat guano
- **Processing:** Refining required
- **Strategic:** CRITICAL if gunpowder technology exists

### SULFUR
- **Location:** Volcanic areas, hot springs
- **Collection:** Surface deposits, mining
- **Uses:** Medicine, bleaching, gunpowder (if exists)
- **Smell:** Distinctive rotten egg smell
- **Cultural:** Associated with evil, demons, hell
- **Alchemy:** Used in early chemistry experiments

## STRATEGIC MATERIALS

### MERCURY (Quicksilver)
- **Source:** Cinnabar ore (mercury sulfide)
- **Location:** Rare deposits in volcanic regions
- **Properties:** Liquid metal at room temperature
- **Uses:** Gilding, alchemy, medicine (dangerous), mining (gold amalgam)
- **Value:** HIGH - Rare and useful
- **Toxicity:** Dangerous to mine and use
- **Cultural:** Associated with transformation, magic

### ALUM
- **Uses:** Mordant (fixes dyes), leather tanning, medicine
- **Location:** Specific volcanic/hydrothermal areas
- **Processing:** Mined or manufactured
- **Trade:** Valuable, controlled by those with access
- **Textile Industry:** CRITICAL for dyeing

### BORAX
- **Location:** Desert salt lakes, hot springs
- **Uses:** Flux (metalworking), cleaning, glazes
- **Occurrence:** Seasonal deposits in desert playas
- **Collection:** After water evaporates
- **Value:** Moderate, useful material

## AGRICULTURAL RESOURCES

### FERTILE SOIL TYPES

**ALLUVIAL SOIL (River Valleys)**
- **Location:** Floodplains of major rivers
- **Quality:** Rich, renewed by annual floods
- **Uses:** Prime agricultural land
- **Crops:** Wheat, vegetables, orchards
- **Value:** Most valuable agricultural land

**LOESS (Wind-Deposited Silt)**
- **Location:** Some lowland areas
- **Quality:** Extremely fertile, easy to work
- **Danger:** Prone to erosion if not protected
- **Uses:** Grain production

**FOREST SOIL**
- **Location:** Cleared forest areas
- **Quality:** Rich in organic matter when first cleared
- **Decline:** Productivity drops without management
- **Amendment:** Needs fertilizer after initial clearing

**VOLCANIC SOIL**
- **Location:** Near ancient volcanic areas (rare)
- **Quality:** Extremely fertile, mineral-rich
- **Advantage:** Long-lasting fertility
- **Crops:** Supports intensive agriculture

### FERTILIZERS

**MANURE**
- **Sources:** Horses, cattle, sheep, chickens (different properties)
- **Uses:** Soil amendment, enrichment
- **Composting:** Aged before application
- **Value:** Essential for sustained agriculture

**GUANO**
- **Source:** Bat caves, seabird colonies
- **Quality:** High nitrogen, phosphorus
- **Value:** Extremely valuable if available
- **Trade:** Worth transporting long distances

**BONE MEAL**
- **Source:** Ground animal bones
- **Uses:** Phosphorus source, slow-release fertilizer
- **Processing:** Ground in mills
- **Availability:** Byproduct of butchering

**GREEN MANURE**
- **Method:** Growing crops (clover, peas) to plow under
- **Benefit:** Nitrogen fixation, organic matter
- **Practice:** Sophisticated farming technique

## WATER RESOURCES

### MOUNTAIN SPRINGS
- **Quality:** Pure, cold, reliable
- **Uses:** Drinking water, irrigation source
- **Settlement:** Villages built around springs
- **Sacred:** Many springs considered holy
- **Diversion:** Engineering brings water to settlements

### RIVERS
- **Major Systems:** Multiple river systems drain mountains
- **Uses:** Transportation, irrigation, power (mills), fishing
- **Flooding:** Annual floods can be destructive or beneficial
- **Control:** Dams, levees, channels (where technology exists)
- **Rights:** Water rights major source of conflict

### GROUNDWATER
- **Aquifers:** Underground water-bearing rocks
- **Wells:** Dug or drilled to access
- **Desert Oases:** Surface expression of groundwater
- **Depth:** Varies greatly by location
- **Recharge:** Dependent on rainfall patterns

### LAKES
- **Types:** Natural (glacial, tectonic), artificial (dammed)
- **Uses:** Fishing, transportation, irrigation reservoir
- **Eutrophication:** Some become overly fertile (algae)
- **Cultural:** Often associated with spirits, legends

## MARINE RESOURCES (Coastal Regions)

### FISH & SEAFOOD
- **Species:** Dozens of commercial species
- **Preservation:** Salting, drying, smoking
- **Trade:** Dried fish exported inland
- **Industry:** Major economic activity for coastal kingdoms
- **Rights:** Fishing rights controlled, conflicts occur

### SHELLFISH
- **Types:** Clams, mussels, oysters, crabs
- **Harvesting:** Tidal zones, deep water
- **Uses:** Food, shells (ornament, tools, lime)
- **Pearls:** Rare, extremely valuable
- **Purple Dye:** From specific mollusks (most valuable dye)

### SEAWEED
- **Uses:** Food (some species), fertilizer, gel extraction
- **Harvest:** Gathered from shore or boats
- **Processing:** Dried for preservation
- **Abundance:** Renewable resource

### AMBER
- **Origin:** Fossilized tree resin, washes up on shores
- **Collection:** Gathered after storms
- **Uses:** Jewelry, magical properties attributed
- **Value:** HIGH - Golden color, inclusions of insects prized
- **Trade:** Exported far inland

## MANUFACTURED MATERIALS

### GLASS
- **Ingredients:** Sand (silica), soda ash, lime
- **Production:** High-temperature furnaces (1400+°C)
- **Types:** Bottles, windows (small panes), beads, lenses
- **Quality:** Clear glass difficult, most is greenish
- **Centers:** Glass-making concentrated in specific regions
- **Value:** Windows expensive, mark of wealth

### CERAMICS
- **Types:** Earthenware, stoneware, porcelain (rare)
- **Production:** Potter's wheels, kilns
- **Glazes:** Lead, salt, ash glazes
- **Uses:** Storage, cooking, decoration, tiles
- **Art:** Some centers produce highly artistic pieces

### LEATHER
- **Sources:** Cattle, horse, pig, sheep, goat hides
- **Tanning:** Vegetable tanning (oak bark), alum tanning
- **Types:** Sole leather (thick), garment leather (thin)
- **Uses:** Clothing, armor, bags, bindings, harness
- **Quality:** Varies greatly by source animal and tanning

### TEXTILES
- **Fibers:** Wool, linen, hemp, nettle, cotton (if imported)
- **Processing:** Spinning, weaving, dyeing, fulling
- **Types:** From coarse sacking to fine cloth
- **Industry:** Major industry in every settled region
- **Guild Control:** Often controlled by powerful guilds

## RARE & EXOTIC MATERIALS

### IVORY
- **Source:** Elephant tusks (if elephants exist), walrus, narwhal
- **Rarity:** EXTREME - Must be imported if not local
- **Uses:** Carving, inlay, luxury items, dice
- **Value:** Weight in gold or more
- **Trade:** Status symbol

### SILK (If imported from beyond Aridonia)
- **Source:** Not native to Aridonia
- **Value:** EXTREME - Ultimate luxury fabric
- **Uses:** Clothing for nobility, banners, religious vestments
- **Trade:** Major reason for long-distance trade

### SPICES (If imported)
- **Types:** Pepper, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, saffron
- **Rarity:** Most must be imported
- **Value:** EXTREME - Worth weight in silver or gold
- **Uses:** Food, medicine, perfume, preservation
- **Trade:** Drive long-distance commerce

### DYES
- **Purple (Tyrian):** From marine snails, most expensive color
- **Indigo:** Imported or grown in warm regions
- **Madder:** Red dye, grown locally
- **Woad:** Blue dye, grown locally
- **Saffron:** Most expensive dye by weight
- **Kermes:** Insect-based red, expensive
