# ARIDONIA - RESOURCES, MINERALS & ELEMENTS COMPENDIUM
## Complete Survey of Natural Resources, Minerals, and Strategic Materials

---

## OVERVIEW

Aridonia's resource distribution reflects its dramatic geological history. The central mountain range, formed by ancient tectonic collision, hosts rich mineral deposits. Western maritime regions provide timber and agricultural wealth, while eastern deserts contain unique minerals and fossil resources. The continent's diverse geology creates regional specialization and drives inter-regional trade.

**Resource Provinces:**
1. Western Forests (Timber, Agriculture)
2. Mountain Ranges (Metals, Gems)
3. Eastern Desert (Rare minerals, Fossils)
4. Coastal Zones (Salt, Fish)
5. Underground (Deep minerals, Geothermal)

## METALLIC RESOURCES

### IRON & STEEL

#### **Ayarson Iron Deposits**
- **Location:** Ayarson mountains (Dwarven City region)
- **Type:** Hematite and magnetite ore bodies
- **Grade:** High (55-68% iron content)
- **Accessibility:** Underground mines, some open pit
- **Production:**
  - Primary source of iron for western archipelago
  - Dwarven smiths produce exceptional steel
  - Annual output: ~50,000 tons ore
- **Mining History:**
  - Continuous mining for 1,500+ years
  - Deep workings extend 800+ meters
  - Some mines considered "played out"
- **Cultural Significance:**
  - Dwarven identity tied to ironworking
  - "Ayarson Steel" = premium brand
  - Secret forging techniques (clan knowledge)
- **Trade Routes:**
  - Ore shipped to FylloDraya for distribution
  - Finished goods traded empire-wide
- **Strategic Importance:** CRITICAL - military applications

#### **Rust Iron Sands**
- **Location:** Northern beaches, Kingdom of Rust
- **Type:** Magnetite beach sands
- **Grade:** Medium (30-40% iron)
- **Accessibility:** Surface collection, easily mined
- **History:**
  - Kingdom name derived from rust-colored beaches
  - Historically important, now depleted
  - Most accessible deposits exhausted
- **Current Status:** Minor supplementary source
- **Environmental Impact:** Beach erosion from over-mining

### COPPER

#### **Visselet Copper**
- **Location:** Fort Visselet region, volcanic rocks
- **Type:** Chalcopyrite, malachite, azurite
- **Grade:** Medium to high (1.5-4% copper)
- **Uses:**
  - Bronze making (with tin)
  - Coinage
  - Decorative metalwork
  - Roofing (wealthy buildings)
- **Production:** ~5,000 tons annually
- **Characteristics:**
  - Green malachite very decorative
  - Some deposits near surface
  - Deep mines in volcanic rock
- **Cultural Uses:**
  - Copper roofs = wealth symbol
  - Green patina prized aesthetically

### TIN

#### **Prauka Tin Streams**
- **Location:** Ruins of Prauka region
- **Type:** Cassiterite in stream gravels (placer deposits)
- **Grade:** Variable (0.01-1% concentrated in gravels)
- **Historical Importance:**
  - Ancient Prauka prospered from tin trade
  - Essential for bronze age civilization
  - Contributed to region's wealth
- **Current Status:**
  - Most easy deposits exhausted
  - Some small-scale artisanal mining continues
  - Ruins contain abandoned mine workings
- **Bronze Production:**
  - Combined with Visselet copper
  - Critical military alloy
- **Scarcity:** Increasing, prices rising

### PRECIOUS METALS

#### **Mountain Gold**
- **Location:** Multiple sites in Dividing Highlands
- **Type:** Placer (stream) and lode (vein) deposits
- **Richest Sites:**
  - **Vorth Loge Gold Fields:** Significant placer deposits in streams
  - **Ayarson Deep Gold:** Vein gold in quartz, deep mines
  - **Hidden valleys:** Many small deposits in remote areas
- **Production:** ~1,000 kg annually (continent-wide)
- **Cultural Significance:**
  - Basis of multiple currencies
  - Religious/ceremonial objects
  - Wealth storage
  - Status symbol
- **Panning vs. Mining:**
  - Placer: accessible to prospectors, low capital
  - Lode: requires significant infrastructure
- **Claims System:**
  - Complex laws, varies by kingdom
  - Source of disputes, wars
  - Mining guilds control major operations

#### **Desert Silver**
- **Location:** Eastern deserts, Vorth Empire
- **Type:** Native silver in volcanic rocks, galena (lead-silver)
- **Occurrence:** Scattered deposits, often in ancient volcanic areas
- **Uses:**
  - Coinage (silver standard in Vorth Empire)
  - Jewelry, ornaments
  - Religious objects
  - Mirrors (polished silver)
- **Production:** ~500 kg annually
- **Notable:**
  - Often associated with lead deposits
  - Some extremely high-grade pockets discovered
  - Prospecting in desert challenging but profitable

### RARE METALS

#### **Ironwood Metal** (Ferrum Lignum)
- **Location:** Unique to Rust Ironwood trees, Kingdom of Rust
- **Type:** Biological metal accumulation
- **Characteristics:**
  - Trees extract iron from soil, concentrate in wood
  - Metal content 5-15% by weight
  - Chemically unusual (organic iron compounds)
- **Uses:**
  - Historical: armor, weapons (wood-metal composite)
  - Modern: decorative items, curiosities
  - Alchemical: ingredient in various preparations
- **Status:** CRITICALLY RARE
  - Tree species nearly extinct from overharvesting
  - Protected (cutting prohibited)
  - Historical items extremely valuable
- **Scientific Interest:**
  - Unique bioaccumulation process
  - Studied by alchemists and scholars

### BASE METALS

#### **Lead**
- **Location:** Various sites, often with silver
- **Uses:**
  - Roofing, plumbing
  - Weights, ballast
  - Pewter (lead-tin alloy)
  - Pigments (white lead)
- **Production:** ~20,000 tons annually
- **Health Concerns:** Toxicity known, regulations in cities

#### **Zinc**
- **Location:** Scattered deposits
- **Uses:**
  - Brass (copper-zinc alloy)
  - Galvanizing iron (rust prevention)
  - Medicinal compounds
- **Production:** ~8,000 tons annually

## GEMSTONES & PRECIOUS MINERALS

### PRECIOUS GEMS

#### **Cerulea Sapphires**
- **Location:** Kingdom of Cerulea, highland gravels
- **Color:** Deep blue (namesake of kingdom)
- **Quality:** High clarity, large crystals (up to 50 carats rough)
- **Formation:** From eroded volcanic rocks, concentrated in streams
- **Mining:**
  - Artisanal panning and digging
  - Some underground mining in source rocks
- **Cultural Significance:**
  - Kingdom of Cerulea heraldic gem
  - Royal family crown contains 200-carat stone
  - "Blue as Cerulea sapphires" = color standard
- **Trade:**
  - Major export, high value
  - Famous across Vivestera
  - Some counterfeits (blue glass) in markets
- **Annual Production:** ~50kg gem-quality

#### **Ayarson Emeralds**
- **Location:** Ayarson mountains, metamorphic rocks
- **Color:** Deep green to bluish-green
- **Quality:** Often included (internal flaws), but some exceptional
- **Formation:** In metamorphic schists, difficult mining
- **Uses:**
  - Jewelry (especially dwarven crafted)
  - Religious objects
  - Status symbols
- **Dwarven Culture:**
  - Master gem cutters
  - "Ayarson cut" = specific faceting style
  - Some clans specialize in emerald work
- **Production:** ~20kg annually

#### **Desert Rubies**
- **Location:** Vorth Empire, marble deposits
- **Color:** Deep red ("blood red")
- **Quality:** Variable, some very fine
- **Mythology:**
  - Called "Dragon's Blood Crystals"
  - Said to form from dragon blood (folklore)
  - Prized by Vorth nobility
- **Uses:**
  - Imperial regalia
  - Ceremonial weapons (pommel stones)
  - Trade goods
- **Production:** ~15kg annually

### SEMI-PRECIOUS GEMS

#### **Prauka Amethyst**
- **Location:** Ruins of Prauka region
- **Color:** Purple, various shades
- **Occurrence:** Geodes in volcanic rocks
- **Historical:**
  - Ancient Prauka famous for amethyst trade
  - Ruins contain many carved amethyst objects
  - "Prauka Purple" = specific deep shade
- **Current:**
  - Still found by ruin-scavengers
  - Quality variable
  - Historical pieces worth more than new
- **Cultural:**
  - Associated with nobility (historical)
  - Wine goblets (prevents drunkenness - folklore)

#### **Loge Crystal**
- **Location:** Vorth Loge, high altitude caves
- **Type:** Clear quartz, exceptionally pure
- **Size:** Some crystals 1+ meter long
- **Uses:**
  - Optical (lenses, prisms)
  - Magical focuses (claimed)
  - Decorative
  - Scientific instruments
- **Mining:**
  - Dangerous (high altitude, cave-ins)
  - Limited access (military controlled)
- **Properties:**
  - Extremely clear, minimal inclusions
  - Some show unusual optical effects
  - Valued by wizards, scholars

### ORNAMENTAL STONES

#### **Rust Agate**
- **Location:** Kingdom of Rust, ancient lava flows
- **Appearance:** Banded red, orange, rust colors
- **Uses:**
  - Decorative objects
  - Beads, cameos
  - Building stone (polished panels)
- **Abundance:** Common
- **Cultural:** Kingdom of Rust iconic stone

#### **Bitter Marble**
- **Location:** BitterMarsh region, metamorphic outcrops
- **Colors:** White, gray, green-veined
- **Quality:** Fine grain, takes polish well
- **Uses:**
  - Sculpture
  - Building (temples, palaces)
  - Monuments
- **Famous:** BitterMarsh monuments (ancient)
- **Quarries:** Some active, some abandoned

## FOSSIL FUELS & COMBUSTIBLES

### COAL

#### **Westallis Coal Fields**
- **Location:** Northern Kingdom of Westallis
- **Type:** Bituminous coal (high quality)
- **Depth:** Some surface, some deep (500m+)
- **Formation:** Ancient swamp forests, buried and compressed
- **Uses:**
  - Heating fuel (especially cities)
  - Metalworking (high temperatures)
  - Smithing, forging
  - Steam power (if technology develops)
- **Production:** ~100,000 tons annually (increasing)
- **Mining:**
  - Dangerous (cave-ins, gas, dust)
  - Underground mining dominates
  - Child labor issues (being addressed)
- **Environmental:**
  - Smoke pollution in cities
  - Deforestation reduced by coal use

### PEAT

#### **BitterMarsh Peat Bogs**
- **Location:** BitterMarsh wetlands
- **Type:** Partially decomposed plant matter
- **Uses:**
  - Fuel (when dried)
  - Soil amendment
  - Preservative properties
- **Extraction:**
  - Cut in blocks, dried
  - Labor-intensive
  - Seasonal (summer drying)
- **Production:** ~10,000 tons annually
- **Cultural:**
  - Traditional fuel for poor
  - "Bog bodies" occasionally found (preserved corpses)

### OIL SEEPS

#### **Desert Oil Springs**
- **Location:** Vorth Empire, scattered seeps
- **Type:** Natural petroleum seepage
- **Uses:**
  - Waterproofing (boats, leather)
  - Medicine (external only)
  - Lamp fuel (smelly, smoky)
  - Lubricant
- **Collection:**
  - Surface scraping
  - Small wells (shallow)
- **Volume:** Limited, ~100 barrels annually
- **Future Potential:** Vast reserves likely underground (undiscovered)

- **Location:** Ayarson mountains
- **Colors:** Gray, pink, black
- **Properties:** Extremely hard, durable
- **Uses:**
  - Foundations, major structures
  - Monuments (lasts millennia)
  - Decorative (polished)
- **Quarrying:**
  - Dwarven expertise
  - Large blocks (precise cutting)

- **Location:** Various regions, especially western islands
- **Uses:**
  - Building stone
  - Cement/mortar (burned)
  - Agricultural lime
  - Sculptures
- **Abundance:** Common
- **Quality:** Variable

#### **Sandstone**
- **Location:** Desert regions
- **Colors:** Red, yellow, tan
- **Properties:** Soft, easy to carve
- **Uses:**
  - Building (dry climates only)
  - Sculpture
  - Decorative

#### **Debrica Clay Beds**
- **Location:** Kingdom of Debrica, river valleys
- **Type:** High-quality pottery clay
- **Colors:** Red, gray when fired
- **Uses:**
  - Pottery (utilitarian and fine)
  - Bricks, tiles
  - Sculpture
- **Production:** Major industry
- **Famous:**
  - "Debrica ware" pottery style
  - Export commodity

## CHEMICAL & INDUSTRIAL MINERALS

### SALT

#### **Vorth Salt Flats**
- **Location:** Desert basins, dried lakes
- **Type:** Halite (rock salt), surface crusts
- **Extraction:** Surface collection
- **Volume:** Virtually unlimited
- **Uses:**
  - Food preservation
  - Seasoning
  - Medicine
  - Trade commodity
  - De-icing (in northern regions)
  - Currency (historically)
- **Quality:** High purity
- **Trade:**
  - Major export from Vorth Empire
  - Traded for forest products
  - Camel caravans main transport
- **Cultural:**
  - "Worth one's salt" = valuable
  - Salt trade enriched empire

#### **Sea Salt**
- **Location:** All coastal regions
- **Production:** Evaporation ponds
- **Quality:** Good, contains minerals
- **Uses:** Similar to rock salt
- **Competition:** Cheaper than inland in coastal areas

### SULFUR

#### **Volcanic Sulfur**
- **Location:** Volcanic regions (Ayarson area, volcanic islands)
- **Type:** Native sulfur deposits
- **Uses:**
  - Gunpowder component (if developed)
  - Medicine (skin conditions)
  - Fumigation
  - Alchemy
- **Collection:** Surface deposits, fumaroles
- **Production:** Small scale

### NITRATES

#### **Desert Nitrates**
- **Location:** Arid caves, dry areas
- **Type:** Various nitrate salts
- **Uses:**
  - Fertilizer
  - Gunpowder component (if developed)
  - Food preservation
- **Collection:** Cave scraping, surface crusts
- **Production:** Limited

## AGRICULTURAL RESOURCES

### ARABLE LAND

**High Quality:**
- Western archipelago river valleys
- Kingdom of Debrica lowlands
- Cerulea coastal plains
- Northern Westallis

**Medium Quality:**
- Highland valleys
- Forest clearings
- Transitional zones

**Low Quality:**
- Desert oases (small, intensive)
- Mountain slopes (terraced)

**Total Arable:** ~15% of continental area

### SOIL TYPES

#### **Forest Soils**
- **Type:** Brown forest soils, podzols
- **Fertility:** Moderate (acidic)
- **Improvements:** Liming, manuring
- **Crops:** Grains, roots, fruit

#### **Grassland Soils**
- **Type:** Chernozem (black earth)
- **Fertility:** High (naturally rich)
- **Location:** Transitional zones
- **Crops:** Grains especially

#### **Desert Soils**
- **Type:** Aridisols (dry soils)
- **Fertility:** Good if irrigated
- **Limitations:** Water critical
- **Crops:** Dates, specialty crops at oases

### IRRIGATION

**Major Systems:**
- **Vorth Canal Network:** Ancient system at oases
- **River Diversions:** FylloDraya region
- **Qanats:** Underground channels in Vorth Empire
- **Terracing:** Mountain agriculture

**Major Systems:**
- **Fyllo River:** Navigable, irrigation, power
- **Westal Rivers:** Multiple systems, fish, transport
- **Desert Wadis:** Seasonal, flash floods

**Uses:**
- Drinking water

- Transport

- Fishing

### GROUNDWATER

**Western Regions:**
- Abundant, shallow wells
- Springs common
- High quality

**Eastern Desert:**
- Deep aquifers (100-500m)
- Fossil water (non-renewable)
- Critical resource
- Qanats tap deep water

**Management:**
- No coordinated policy
- Overuse in some areas
- Conflicts over access

### OASES

**Vorth Empire Oases:**
- ~50 permanent oases
- Support 100-5,000 people each
- Date palm cultivation
- Trade route stops
- Strategic importance
- Water from deep aquifers or springs

## EXOTIC & MAGICAL RESOURCES

### QUESTIONABLE EXISTENCE

#### **Dragon Scales**
- **Claimed Location:** Deep SasSert Desert
- **Description:** Iridescent, extremely hard scales
- **Properties:**
  - Allegedly armor-making
  - Fire resistant
  - Magical conductivity
- **Reality:** No confirmed specimens, likely myth
- **Market:** Fake "dragon scales" sold to tourists

#### **Mithril** (Dwarven True-Silver)
- **Claimed Location:** Deepest Ayarson mines
- **Description:** Silver-white metal, impossibly light and strong
- **Properties:**
  - Lighter than aluminum
  - Stronger than steel
  - Non-tarnishing
- **Reality:** 
  - Possible it exists (dwarves secretive)
  - Never seen outside dwarven hands
  - May be refined aluminum (very difficult to produce)
- **Status:** UNCONFIRMED

#### **Liquid Moonlight**
- **Claimed Location:** Sprout Kingdom, collected on certain nights
- **Description:** Luminescent liquid from plants
- **Properties:**
  - Alchemical catalyst
  - Illumination
  - Healing properties (claimed)
- **Reality:** Likely bioluminescent sap, rare but real
- **Status:** EXISTS but extremely rare

#### **Vorth-Heart Crystal**
- **Claimed Location:** Desert heart, SasSert
- **Description:** Red crystal, pulses like heartbeat
- **Properties:**
  - Grants draconic powers
  - Focus for powerful magic
  - Immortality (extreme claims)
- **Reality:** Likely mythical
- **Searches:** Many expeditions, none successful
- **Status:** MYTHICAL (probably)

## GEOLOGICAL HAZARDS & RESOURCES

### EARTHQUAKES

- **Active Zones:** Mountain regions, volcanic areas
- **Frequency:** Moderate to high in mountains
- **Mining Impact:** Cave-ins, danger to deep mines

### VOLCANOES

- **Status:** Some dormant, occasional activity
- **Resources:** Sulfur, fertile soils, geothermal
- **Hazards:** Rare eruptions, gas emissions

### GEOTHERMAL

- **Locations:** Volcanic regions
- **Uses:**
  - Hot springs (bathing, healing)
  - Heating (limited)
  - Potential power (undeveloped)
