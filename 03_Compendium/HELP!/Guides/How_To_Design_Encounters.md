# How To Design Combat Encounters

**Step 1: The Objective**
Combat should rarely be "kill everything to win."
*   *Defend:* Protect an NPC or object for X rounds.
*   *Escape:* Reach the exit before the roof collapses.
*   *Retrieve:* Grab the idol and run.
*   *Stop:* Prevent the ritual from completing.

**Step 2: The Terrain**
A flat room is boring. Add:
*   **Cover:** Pillars, overturned tables, trees.
*   **Hazards:** Lava pits, spike traps, slippery ice.
*   **Verticality:** Balconies, cliffs, chandeliers can be swung from.

**Step 3: The Monsters**
Mix unit types for synergy.
*   **Brutes:** High HP, low AC. Meat shields. (e.g., Ogre)
*   **Strikers:** High damage, low HP. Glass cannons. (e.g., Assassin)
*   **Artillery:** Ranged attackers. (e.g., Archer, Mage)
*   **Controllers:** Change the battlefield. (e.g., Spellcaster with *Web*)

**Step 4: The Twist**
Change the battle halfway through (Round 3 is usually good).
*   Reinforcements arrive.
*   The terrain changes (bridge collapses).
*   A third party attacks both sides.

**Step 5: Balancing**
*   Don't just add more enemies. Action Economy is king.
*   If the party is winning too easily, add a wave of minions (1 HP enemies).
