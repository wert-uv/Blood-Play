## [[1_Before Fracture]]

### **The Great Separation**

**What Happened:**
A catastrophic energy imbalance occurred when too much Zenith energy tried to flow into Vivestera simultaneously with too much Perdition energy trying to flow upward from below. The layers, still partially connected, violently separated.

**The Fracturing Event:**
- Lasted for 3 days and 3 nights
- Reality itself seemed to tear
- Massive energy discharges across all layers
- Thousands of beings caught between layers during separation

**Immediate Consequences:**
- **Vivestera:** Cataclysmic earthquakes, tsunamis, volcanic eruptions; ~60% of mortal population dies
- **Nirvana:** Becomes distinct realm; those trapped there cannot easily return
- **Tophet:** Stabilizes into consistent elemental chaos
- **Aethra/Abyss:** Solidify as bridge layers (pathways remain but are one-way)
- **Umbra:** Firmly establishes as transition zone
- **Zenith/Perdition:** Seal themselves (now mostly dormant energy sources)

**The Trapped:**
Beings caught between layers during Fracturing:
- Mortals trapped in Nirvana (including young Sereth)
- Mortals trapped in Tophet (including future Seekers)
- Some fell to Umbra, Gehenna, or Abyss
- Most died; some transformed; few returned

**Dating System Established:**
Survivors begin counting years from this event. Year 0 FE becomes universal reference point.

## [[5_Era Of War]]

### **1-100 FE: The Dark Century**

**Vivestera:**
- Survivors struggle to rebuild
- Many civilizations collapse completely
- Knowledge lost; technology regresses
- Warlords and petty kingdoms emerge
- Life expectancy drops to ~30 years

**Nirvana:**
- Trapped mortals begin transformation into celestial beings
- Process is painful and confusing without guidance
- Some die during transformation
- Others adapt and create celestial society
- **~50 FE:** Sereth completes her transformation (one of the earliest)

**Tophet:**
- Trapped mortals must adapt to elemental chaos
- Most die; few survive by bonding with elements
- Future Seekers among survivors
- Dragons emerge as dominant species

**Vivestera:**

## [[6_The New Age]]

### **500-1000 FE: The First Seekers Emerge**

**The Phenomenon of True God:**
Around 500 FE, certain individuals across multiple layers begin reporting visions of an "invisible presence" - a phenomenon they describe as "existence itself made manifest."

**~500 FE - First Sighting (Vivestera):**
- **Tempus** (mortal scholar obsessed with understanding time) sees True God
- Describes it as "the moment between moments, the space where possibility becomes reality"
- Begins following the phenomenon, studying it
- Discovers he can absorb ambient energy from proximity to it

**~520 FE - Second Sighting (Nirvana):**
- **Sereth** encounters True God while guiding souls
- One of few who can perceive it (her obsession with understanding death/transition)
- Begins following when it passes through Nirvana
- Transformation accelerates; gains new powers

**~550 FE - Third Sighting (Vivestera):**
- **Nex** (mortal researcher studying blood/biology) perceives True God
- Her obsession with understanding life makes it visible to her
- Immediately recognizes it as "the pattern underlying creation"
- Dies attempting to get closer; ascends to Nirvana

**~580 FE - Nex in Nirvana:**
- Nex arrives in Nirvana, begins transformation
- Meets Sereth (who guides her)
- Both are following True God phenomenon
- Nex's transformation is... unusual (retains more mortal traits)

**~600 FE - Fourth Sighting (Tophet):**
- **Dravitus** perceives True God moving through elemental chaos
- His mastery of elements makes phenomenon visible
- Begins following, absorbing essence
- Gains ability to manifest in multiple elemental forms

**~620 FE - Fifth Sighting (Tophet):**
- **Imperion** sees True God while attempting to impose order on chaos
- His obsession with control/understanding makes it visible
- Begins following, studying the pattern of existence
- Gains insight into fundamental ordering principles

**~650 FE - Sixth Sighting (Vivestera):**
- **Vorgath** (tribal champion communing with beasts) perceives True God in deep wilderness
- His connection to primal nature makes it visible
- Mortally wounded shortly after; dies and ascends
- Continues following in Nirvana

**~700 FE - Seventh Sighting (Vivestera):**
- **Aevita** (cyborg experimenter pushing boundaries of life) perceives True God
- Her obsession with creation/genesis makes it visible
- Begins following, recording observations
- First to theorize it's "existence itself"

---

### **700-1000 FE: The Gathering**

**The Seekers Begin to Meet:**

**~750 FE - First Meeting (Nirvana):**
- Sereth, Nex, and Vorgath encounter each other while following True God
- Realize they're all perceiving the same phenomenon
- Begin traveling together (uneasy alliance)
- Nex's experiments already disturbing the others

**~800 FE - Aethra Bridge Crossing:**
- True God phenomenon moves from Vivestera through Aethra to Nirvana
- Tempus and Aevita follow from Vivestera side
- First time mortals successfully traverse Aethra
- They emerge in Nirvana transformed (absorbing massive bridge energy)

**~850 FE - Tophet Delegation:**
- True God moves through Abyss to Tophet
- Dravitus and Imperion already following
- Others attempt to follow but Abyss is more dangerous than Aethra
- Only Nex successfully crosses (her biology adapts)
- Groups separated across layers for decades

**~900 FE - The Convergence:**
- True God phenomenon moves to Umbra (neutral ground between all layers)
- All seven followers converge for first time
- Recognize each other as fellow Seekers
- Realize they're all absorbing True God's essence, growing in power
- Name themselves "The Seven Seekers of Enlightenment"

**~950 FE - The Pact:**
- Seekers agree to travel together
- Each pursues understanding of True God through different lens
- Share discoveries and insights
- Tensions already forming (philosophical differences)

---

### **1000-1500 FE: The Ascension**

**The Seekers Become Gods:**

As they continue following True God and absorbing its essence, the Seekers transform:

**~1000 FE:**
- All seven now possess power rivaling lesser deities
- Mortals in Vivestera begin to notice their influence
- Prayers/worship directed at them start accumulating power
- First temples built in their names

**~1100 FE:**
- Nex's transformation complete: Frozen at child-like age but with godlike power
- Her experiments in Nirvana become increasingly extreme
- Sereth's concern growing but True God presence keeps peace

**~1200 FE:**
- Seekers now universally recognized as gods by mortals
- Major religions forming around each
- Divine power feedback loop: worship increases power, power increases worship
- Some Seekers embrace this (Dravitus, Imperion), others resist (Vorgath, Nex)

**~1300 FE:**
- Philosophical tensions rising
- Nex argues worship corrupts the seeking
- Imperion argues power enables understanding
- Tempus observes but doesn't choose sides

**~1400 FE:**
- True God phenomenon begins withdrawing
- Appears less frequently
- When it appears, it's more distant/untouchable
- Seekers debate meaning: Test? Abandonment? Natural cycle?

**~1500 FE - The Departure:**
True God phenomenon disappears completely. Last known presence in Umbra. Left no message, no explanation, no promise of return.

**The Question:**
- Did they fail some test?
- Was this always going to happen?
- Did absorbing its essence somehow push it away?
- Will it return?

## [[3_Dawn of Gods]]

### **1500-1800 FE: The Fracturing (Philosophical)**

Without True God to mediate, Seekers' differences intensify:

**~1520 FE - First Major Disagreement:**
- Aevita begins reshaping Vivestera ecosystems
- Creates "optimal" life forms
- Nex demands she stop (corrupting natural biological patterns)
- Vorgath sides with Nex (natural > artificial)
- Aevita continues with Imperion's support

**~1550 FE - Sabotage Incident:**
- Nex's followers destroy one of Aevita's creation-forges
- Forge malfunction creates abominations
- Aevita retaliates with anti-blood-magic weapons
- First direct Seeker-vs-Seeker conflict

**~1600 FE - The Split:**
Two factions emerge:
- **Naturalists:** Nex, Sereth, Vorgath (enlightenment through understanding natural forces)
- **Artificers:** Aevita, Imperion, Dravitus (enlightenment through mastery and creation)
- **Neutral:** Tempus (enlightenment through observation)

**~1647 FE - THE SUNDERING (First God War):**

**The Conflict:**
- Full-scale war between Naturalist and Artificer factions
- Lasted 47 years
- Millions of mortals died in collateral damage
- Entire regions of Vivestera devastated
- Both sides use mortal champions, divine weapons, layer-manipulation

**Key Battles:**
- **Battle of Forge Prime:** Dravitus vs. Vorgath (elemental vs. primal) - draw
- **The Siege of Blood Temple:** Aevita's constructs vs. Nex's blood army - Nex victory
- **The Cascade Incident:** Imperion tries to control timeline; Tempus stops him - neutral
- **Final Confrontation (1647 FE):** Sereth vs. Imperion in Umbra - interrupted by Tempus

**~1647 FE - Tempus's Intervention:**
- Shows both sides visions of possible futures
- If Naturalists win: Technology stagnates, civilization collapses
- If Artificers win: Nature replaced entirely, life becomes hollow
- If war continues: Vivestera destroyed, enlightenment impossible for anyone

**The Pact of Coexistence (1647 FE):**
All seven Seekers agree:
1. No direct interference with another Seeker's methodology
2. No using mortal armies against another Seeker
3. Conflicting philosophies allowed but not enforced
4. Umbra declared neutral ground for disputes
5. If one Seeker threatens existence of Vivestera, others may unite against them

**Aftermath:**
- Peace holds... for 500 years
- Seekers establish separate domains
- Mortals rebuild from devastation
- Religious divisions deepen (followers reflect Seekers' conflicts)

### **1800-2100 FE: Nex's Descent**

**~1800 FE - Nex's Experiments Escalate:**
- Begins mass sacrifices to study blood patterns
- Creates Blood Crystals (fragments of her essence) as research tools
- Experiments on sentient beings without consent
- Catalog of blood samples reaches millions
- Other Seekers growing concerned but Pact prevents intervention

**~1850 FE - The Blood Temples:**
- Nex establishes temples across Vivestera
- Blood Cult forms (fanatical worshippers)
- Entire bloodlines sacrificed for "research"
- Creates blood abominations (failed experiments)
- Mortal kingdoms begin banning Nex worship

**~1900 FE - The Unforgivable Act:**
**Incident: The Soul Erasure Experiments**

Nex becomes obsessed with understanding how souls form and transform. In Nirvana, she begins experiments:
- Intercepts souls mid-transformation
- Attempts to stop transformation to "study the moment"
- First several subjects: souls damaged but recover
- Later subjects: souls don't recover
- **Final experiment:** Soul completely erased (true death, impossible death)

**Sereth's Discovery:**
- Sereth discovers what Nex has been doing
- Confronts her
- Nex is unrepentant: "The data was invaluable. One soul for infinite understanding."
- **This is when their absolute hatred begins**

**~1950 FE - The Summoning Attempts:**
- Nex concludes: To achieve enlightenment, must study True God itself
- Begins attempting to summon True God
- Creates rituals, sacrifices thousands
- Believes if she can capture and dissect True God, she'll understand everything

**~2050 FE - The Other Seekers Learn:**
- Tempus detects temporal anomalies (Nex trying to pull True God through time)
- Alerts others
- All six realize: Nex will destroy everything in pursuit of knowledge
- To threaten True God is to threaten source of their power/purpose

**~2090 FE - The Decision:**
All six other Seekers meet in Umbra (first time since Sundering):
- Sereth: "She destroyed my charges. She'll destroy us all."
- Vorgath: "She has forgotten that life must be respected."
- Aevita: "I will create the prison."
- Imperion: "I will design the binding."
- Dravitus: "I will provide the power."
- Tempus: "I will ensure the timing is perfect."

**~2100 FE - THE BLOOD GOD WAR:**

**The Alliance:**
For the only time in history, six Seekers unite with single purpose: Stop Nex.

**Preparation (2095-2100 FE):**
- **Aevita** designs "The Cage of Stillness" (dimensional prison)
- **Imperion** creates binding wards (complex mathematical/magical formulae)
- **Dravitus** gathers power from all elements
- **Vorgath** tracks Nex's movements through blood-signatures
- **Sereth** coordinates from Umbra (knows Nex's patterns)
- **Tempus** calculates optimal moment to strike

**The Battle (2100 FE - exactly 1,747 years ago from present):**

**Day 1 - The Assault:**
- Six Seekers attack Nex's primary temple (deep in Aridonia)
- Nex calls blood army: thousands of constructs, cultists, abominations
- Battle destroys entire region (now called Blood Wastes)
- Nex fights desperately but alone

**Day 2 - The Fragmentation:**
- Nex realizes she can't win
- Attempts to scatter her essence to survive
- Aevita activates Cage of Stillness
- Prevents full escape but Nex partially succeeds
- Essence fragments into thousands of Blood Crystals

**Day 3 - The Sealing:**
- Six Seekers work together to:
  1. Gather as many fragments as possible (get ~70%)
  2. Seal largest fragment (Nex's consciousness) in pocket dimension
  3. Scatter remaining fragments across Vivestera
  4. Bind the pocket dimension outside normal layer structure

**The Result:**
- Nex's consciousness sealed in "The Nex Void"
- ~300 Blood Crystals remain scattered (30% of her essence)
- Each crystal retains fragment of awareness
- If enough crystals gathered, seal weakens
- If all crystals gathered, Nex returns fully

**The Cost:**
- Millions of mortals killed in collateral damage
- Entire regions corrupted with blood magic
- All six Seekers weakened from effort (took decades to recover)
- Blood Cult survivors go underground
- Nex's name forbidden, temples destroyed

## TIMELINE NOTES

**Dating Inconsistencies:**
Different cultures use different calendars. The "Fracturing Epoch" dating is scholarly standard but not universal:
- Vorth Empire counts from empire founding (1,500+ years)
- Dwarves count from First King (5,000+ years)
- Sylvanic people count seasons, not years
- Some cultures still use Pre-Fracturing mythical dates

**Historical Accuracy:**
- Events before 1000 FE are partially mythological
- Seeker testimony is primary source but biased
- Mortals have fragmented records
- True God's actions/nature still debated

**Prophecy:**
The Blood Moon Prophecy suggests Nex's return is inevitable, but Tempus says he's seen futures where she doesn't return. The prophecy may be self-fulfilling or may be wrong.
