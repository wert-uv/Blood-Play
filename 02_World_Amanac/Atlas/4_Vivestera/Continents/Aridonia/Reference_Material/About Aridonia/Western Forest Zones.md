### WESTERN FOREST ZONES - UNNAMED REGIONS

**Position:** Western areas of the eastern continent, between archipelago and central mountains
**Terrain Type:** Dense temperate forest
**Description:** Extensive forested regions that receive adequate rainfall from western maritime weather patterns. These forests are largely unnamed on the map, suggesting either wilderness, unclaimed territories, or regions controlled by Westallis or Cerulea as frontier zones.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- Multiple large river systems draining westward
- Very dense forest coverage (approaching 100% in some areas)
- Elevation gradually rises moving eastward toward mountains
- Several large lakes in glacially-carved basins
- Minimal visible settlement – true wilderness
- Old growth forest characteristics
- Possible inhabited by indigenous peoples not shown on political map
- Wildlife corridors connecting different forest regions
- Some areas of swamp or marsh in low-lying zones
- Seasonal flooding evident in some river valleys
