# RUSTIC CULTURE (KINGDOM OF RUST)

### OVERVIEW

**Location:** Northern major island, industrial heartland
**Population:** ~800,000
**Lifestyle:** Urban industrial, mining, manufacturing
**Primary Economy:** Metalworking, coal mining, manufacturing
**Social Structure:** Industrial guilds, unions, merchant class

**Core Values:**
- Innovation and progress
- Work ethic (pride in labor)
- Collective action (union solidarity)
- Pragmatism over tradition
- Equality (more than other cultures)

### LANGUAGE: RUSTIC (INDUSTRIAL VERNACULAR)

**Linguistic Characteristics:**
- Simplified from Old Maritime
- Technical vocabulary extensive
- Slang evolves rapidly
- Class differences in dialect (workers vs. bosses)
- Written in simplified script

### CULTURAL PRACTICES

**Naming:**
- Given name + Occupation/Guild
- Example: "William Steelworker"
- Family names less important than trade
- Nicknames very common ("Big John," "Red Mary")

**Labor Culture:**
- 12-hour shifts (being reduced to 10 by union pressure)
- "Forge-Day" annual festival (workers' celebration)
- Strong union solidarity
- Strikes common, sometimes violent
- Apprenticeship system (7 years to master)

**Social Changes:**
- Challenging old nobility
- Merchant and skilled worker class rising
- Education movements (workers' schools)
- Political reforms (representation demands)

# RUSTIC (INDUSTRIAL COMMON)

### **Geographic Distribution:** Kingdom of Rust (Southwestern)
### **Population:** ~400,000
### **Government:** Constitutional Monarchy (Parliament limits king)
### **Primary Language:** Rustic Common (Industrial dialect)

---

### **CULTURAL VALUES & WORLDVIEW**

**Core Values:**
1. **Labor-Dignity (Fair-Work):** Honest work defines worth
2. **Progress (Forward-March):** Tradition can be improved
3. **Equality (Level-Ground):** All workers deserve respect
4. **Innovation (New-Forge):** Embrace new technology
5. **Solidarity (Union-Strong):** Workers united cannot be broken

**Social Structure:**
- **Merchant Princes:** Wealthy industrialists
- **Skilled Workers:** Engineers, smiths, craftspeople
- **Laborers:** Miners, factory workers, farmers
- **Scholars:** Inventors, theorists, educators
- Note: Upward mobility possible through skill/invention

---

### **RUSTIC COMMON LANGUAGE STRUCTURE**

**Phonology:**
- **Practical:** Easy to yell over machinery
- **Simplified:** Fewer complex sounds than High Common
- **Clipped:** Words shortened for efficiency

**Grammar:**
- **Direct:** Simple subject-verb-object
- **Technical Terms:** Many borrowed from Khazdul (mining)

### **PLACE-NAMING EVOLUTION - RUSTIC EXAMPLES**

#### **Example 1: Kingdom of Rust → National Name**

**Step 1 - Original Meaning:**
- **Oxidelands** = "Lands of the rust-colored hills"
- Named by first settlers for reddish iron-stained hills
- Geology: Surface iron oxidation created rust color

**Step 2 - Linguistic Evolution:**
- **Oxidelands** → **Rustlands** (600 years ago)
  - "Oxide" was technical term; "Rust" was common word
  - Simplified for general population
- **Rustlands** → **Kingdom of Rust** (300 years ago)
  - "Kingdom" added when monarchy established
  - Crown claimed legitimacy from land's strength (iron)

**Step 3 - Endonym vs Exonym:**
- **Endonym (Rustic):** "The Rust" (proud; defiant)
- **Formal:** "Kingdom of Rust" (official)
- **Exonym (Nobility elsewhere):** "The Sooty Kingdom" (dismissive)
- **Exonym (Traders):** "The Iron Realm" (respectful)

**Political Evolution:**
- Name initially neutral (descriptive)
- Became source of pride during industrialization
- "Rust" now means "working-class pride" culturally
- Anti-monarchy faction calls it "The People's Rust"

---

#### **Example 2: Ferropolis (Iron City)**

**Step 1 - Original Meaning:**
- **Iron-Town** = Simple descriptive (400 years ago)
- Named when first smelters built
- Just one of many mining settlements

**Step 2 - Linguistic Evolution:**
- **Iron-Town** → **Iron-City** (300 years ago)
  - Grew large enough to be called city
  - Name remained descriptive
- **Iron-City** → **Ferropolis** (150 years ago)
  - Wealthy merchant princes wanted "sophisticated" name
  - Added Greek-style "-polis" suffix
  - "Iron" Latinized to "Ferro"
  - Working class HATED this change

**Step 3 - Endonym vs Exonym:**
- **Endonym (Elite):** "Ferropolis" (trying to sound cultured)
- **Endonym (Working Class):** "Iron-Town" (defiantly kept old name)
- **Compromise:** "Ferro" (shortened; both use it now)
- **Exonym:** "The Iron Capital" (trade name)

**Class Conflict Encoded:**
- Two names represent class divide
- Saying "Ferropolis" marks you as elite/sympathizer
- Saying "Iron-Town" marks you as working class
- "Ferro" is neutral, accepted by both
- Maps show "Ferropolis (Iron-Town)" to acknowledge both

---

#### **Example 3: Slag-Row → Poor District**

**Step 1 - Original Meaning:**
- **Smelter-Waste-District** (descriptive)
- Built on dumping ground for smelting waste
- Originally temporary worker housing

**Step 2 - Linguistic Evolution:**
- **Smelter-Waste-District** → **Slag-Heap** (immediately)
  - Workers shortened it instantly
  - "Smelter-Waste" = "Slag" (technical term)
- **Slag-Heap** → **Slag-Row** (50 years later)
  - "Row" (row houses built)
  - Permanent settlement acknowledged

**Step 3 - Endonym vs Exonym:**
- **Endonym (Residents):** "The Row" (pride in survival)
- **Exonym (City Elite):** "Slag-Row" (dismissive)
- **Official Documents:** "Eastern Workers' District" (euphemism)
- **Revolutionary Name:** "Freedom Row" (proposed; never official)

**Cultural Reclamation:**
- Name originally insult (built on waste)
- Residents reclaimed it with pride
- "Slag-Row born" = tough, resilient (positive identity)
- Community rejects official euphemism
