# CENTRAL MOUNTAIN RANGE - THE DIVIDING HIGHLANDS

**Position:** Running north-south through the center of the continent, separating west from east
**Terrain Type:** Significant mountain system with multiple parallel ridges
**Description:** A major mountain range that creates a dramatic climate and vegetation divide. The mountains run approximately 250-300 kilometers north to south, with peaks reaching estimated 2500-3500 meters based on permanent snow on higher peaks. These mountains are the single most important geographic feature of Aridonia, creating a rain shadow that produces the eastern desert.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- At least 15-20 major peaks visible
- Multiple parallel ridges running north-south
- High mountain passes at several locations (3-4 visible)
- Alpine lakes in high valleys (5-6 visible)
- Glacial valleys with U-shaped profiles
- Western slopes are heavily forested
- Eastern slopes are bare rock and scree
- Several rivers originate in these mountains
- Evidence of past glaciation in valley shapes
- Possible volcanic origin based on peak symmetry

# EASTERN DESERT REGION - VORTH EMPIRE (Western Desert Zone)

**Position:** East of the central mountains, west of the SasSert Desert core
**Terrain Type:** Semi-arid shrubland transitioning to true desert
**Description:** The transitional zone between the mountain rain shadow and the deep desert. This region receives minimal precipitation due to the barrier effect of the mountains. Vegetation consists of drought-resistant shrubs, scattered acacia-type trees, and seasonal grasses. The terrain shows evidence of ancient river courses now dry or seasonal.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- Several dry wadis (seasonal watercourses) run eastward
- Scattered oasis settlements (8-10 small communities)
- Areas of badlands with exposed stratified rock
- Some dune formation in the eastern sections
- Rocky desert pavement in many areas
- A few permanent springs support small settlements
- Ancient dried lake beds visible as salt flats
- Caravan routes cross the region (visible as faint lines)

# SOUTHEASTERN DESERT TRANSITION

**Position:** Southeastern region of the continent
**Terrain Type:** Desert transitioning to semi-arid grassland
**Description:** Where the deep desert begins to receive more moisture from southeastern weather patterns. Vegetation becomes denser with grasses and small trees increasing. Several permanent settlements exist here based on groundwater and strategic trade positions.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- Vegetation density gradually increases eastward
- Several towns positioned along an east-west trade route
- Alluvial fans at the base of isolated mountain ranges
- Seasonal lakes (playas) that fill during rare rain events
- Increased rock outcrop diversity
- Some areas of fossil-rich badlands
- Ancient ruins suggesting this was once more hospitable
