# ARIDONIA - CULTURES, PEOPLES & LINGUISTIC COMPENDIUM
## Comprehensive Cultural Atlas with Historical Lexicons

---

## OVERVIEW

Aridonia hosts at least 12 distinct major cultural groups, each with unique languages, traditions, and worldviews. The dramatic geographic barriers (mountains, deserts, seas) have created isolated cultural evolution, while trade routes have produced linguistic exchanges and cultural blending in certain zones.

**Major Cultural-Linguistic Groups:**
1. Vorthic (Draconic Empire Culture)
2. Dwarven (Ayarson Mountain Clans)
3. Sylvan (Sprout Kingdom Forest Folk)
4. Maritime Western (Island Kingdoms)
5. Rustic (Kingdom of Rust Industrial Culture)
6. Pastoral (Kingdom of Debrica)
7. Ancient Praukai (Ruins culture, largely extinct)
8. Cerulean (Noble Island Culture)

---

## 1. VORTHIC CULTURE (VORTH EMPIRE)

### OVERVIEW

**Location:** Eastern deserts, SasSert Desert, Vorth Empire
**Population:** ~2 million
**Lifestyle:** Desert nomads, oasis city-dwellers, merchants
**Primary Economy:** Salt trade, date cultivation, camel caravans
**Social Structure:** Strictly hierarchical, clan-based

**Core Values:**
- Endurance (survival in harsh environment)
- Honor and lineage
- Hospitality to travelers (sacred law)
- Knowledge preservation
- Draconic symbolism (mythic dragon heritage claimed)

### LANGUAGE: VORTHIC (HIGH DRACONIC)

**Linguistic Characteristics:**
- Guttural consonants (kh, gh, qh)
- Complex verb conjugations (12 tenses)
- Written in angular script (draconic runes)
- Three registers: High (nobility), Mid (merchants), Low (commoners)
- Poetic tradition (oral history preserved in verse)

   - Middle: "Sass-Sertan"

- **Khalzar**: "Sky-water" - Mirage lake (dry, appears wet)

**Exonyms (External names):**
- **The Gilded Cage**: Western name for Kriv-Zal-Aru (wealthy but isolated)
- **Deathsand**: Western name for Mor-Sert region
- **Dragon's Teeth**: Western name for Drak-Mor peaks

### CULTURAL PRACTICES

**Naming Conventions:**
- Personal name + Clan name + Home oasis
- Example: "Sarathek Vorthakresh of Zal-Aru"
- Women keep names after marriage (matrilineal descent tracking)

**Hospitality Law (Dhariv):**
- Any traveler must be offered water, shade, rest
- Host protected by guest for 3 days after departure
- Breaking hospitality = death penalty
- Creates safe travel network

**Salt Ceremony:**
- Newborns touched with salt (preservation, blessing)
- Marriage: Exchange salt (binding)
- Death: Buried with salt pouch (preserve soul)

## 2. DWARVEN CULTURE (AYARSON CLANS)

### OVERVIEW

**Location:** Ayarson mountains, underground cities
**Population:** ~500,000
**Lifestyle:** Underground mining, metalworking, stone carving
**Primary Economy:** Mining, metalwork, trade of finished goods
**Social Structure:** Clan-based, guild system, craft specialization

**Core Values:**
- Craftsmanship excellence
- Clan loyalty
- Stone and metal reverence
- Honor and grudges (both remembered forever)
- Isolationism (but pragmatic trade)

### LANGUAGE: KHAZDUL (MOUNTAIN TONGUE)

**Linguistic Characteristics:**
- Heavy consonant clusters (khr, dwr, ghr)
- Tonal elements (pitch carries meaning)
- Rune-based writing (angular, suited for stone carving)
- Extremely conservative (little change over millennia)
- Secret inner language (clan-specific dialects)

- **Khaz-Uzbad**: "Mountain-Lord's Hall" - Royal residence
- **Mithril-Delve**: "True-Silver Mine" - Forbidden area (outsiders die)

**Exonyms (Surface-dweller names):**
- **Dwarven City of Ayarson**: Human approximation
- **The Ironholds**: Western term for dwarven territory
- **The Deep Vaults**: Reference to underground wealth
- **Hammer-Home**: Rustic poetic name

### CULTURAL PRACTICES

**Naming Conventions:**
- Given name + Clan name + Craft
- Example: "Thorin Ironforge, Rune-Singer"
- Some clans use "Son/Daughter of [Parent]"
- Greatest crafters get work-names: "Goldhand," "Gemcutter"

**Grudge-Keeping:**
- All wrongs recorded in "Grudge-Stones"
- Clan obligation to avenge/balance grudges
- Some grudges centuries old, still active
- Can be settled through compensation, service, or blood

**Craft-Secret Keeping:**
- Greatest techniques never written, only taught
- Some families guard specific secrets (steel tempering, gem cutting)
- Revealing secrets = exile (equivalent to death for dwarf)
- Secret hand signals and code-words among smiths

**Coming of Age:**
- Must craft a masterwork by age 50
- Presented to Dum-Keeper for judgment
- Accepted = full clan member, guild entry
- Rejected = shame, must try again

**Population:** ~200,000 (estimate, many hidden)
**Lifestyle:** Forest-dwelling, harmony with nature

**Social Structure:** Druidic council, no formal kings

**Core Values:**

- Isolation from outsiders
- Ancient tradition keeping

- Soft consonants, flowing vowels
- Extensive plant/animal vocabulary (100+ words for "green")
- Verb-final sentence structure
- Whistling and bird-call elements (forest communication)

### CULTURAL PRACTICES

**Naming Conventions:**
- No family names (individual + descriptor)

- Taking dead wood, fallen branches = permitted
- Hunting: Take only what you need
- Outsider entry: Forbidden (punished by Shadow-Walkers)
- Trial by ordeal for serious crimes (forest "judges")

**Spiritual Practices:**
- Daily meditation under "spirit tree"
- Seasonal festivals aligned with plant cycles
- Grove-Speakers claim to hear tree "voices"
- Ancestor spirits believed to reside in oldest trees
- Dead buried at tree roots (return to cycle)

## 7. ANCIENT PRAUKAI CULTURE (HISTORICAL)

### OVERVIEW

**Location:** Ruins of Prauka (now abandoned)
**Population:** 0 (extinct as distinct culture ~400 years ago)
**Historical Period:** 2000-400 years ago (collapse)
**Historical Economy:** Viticulture, tin trade, art

**Core Values (Historical):**
- Artistic excellence
- Wine culture
- Astronomy/astrology
- Merchant wealth
- Hedonism (according to outsiders)

### LANGUAGE: PRAUKAI (DEAD LANGUAGE)

**Linguistic Characteristics:**
- Complex tense system (past has 8 forms)
- Poetic meter in common speech
- Extensive color vocabulary
- Influenced modern Maritime Common
- Now studied by scholars only

### BASE LEXICON - CULTURAL TERMS

| Praukai | English | Context |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Prauk** | Star/Fate | Original settlement named for star position |
| **Vinum** | Wine/Blood/Life | Central to culture |
| **Tinor** | Tin/Wealth | Source of prosperity |
| **Stelios** | Star-reader | Astronomer/astrologer |
| **Purpur** | Purple/Royal | From shellfish dye |
| **Terras** | Earth/Vineyard | Winemaking land |

### WHY THE COLLAPSE?

**Theories (Scholarly Debate):**
1. Tin deposits exhausted (economic collapse)
2. Climate change (vineyards failed)
3. Plague (population crash)
4. Invasion (no evidence)
5. Decadence (moral judgment, unlikely)
6. Combination of factors (most likely)

**Legacy:**
- Ruins are archaeological site
- Ancient wine cellars still explored
- Prauka Amethyst still found
- Language preserved in inscriptions
- Influenced Maritime Common vocabulary

**Maritime Common:**
- Lingua franca of western archipelago
- Spoken by all merchants
- Simplified grammar
- Most neutral, no prestige

**Vorthic (High):**
- Used in desert trade
- Prestige in Vorth Empire

## WRITING SYSTEMS

**Draconic Runes** (Vorthic)
- Angular, suited for stone/sand
- 24 characters
- Read left-to-right

**Dwarven Runes** (Khazdul)
- Geometric, designed for carving
- 28 characters
- Read left-to-right

**Maritime Script**
- Phonetic alphabet
- 26 characters
- Simplified, easy to learn

**Spiral Script** (Sylvanic)
- Written in spirals (tree-rings)
- 30 characters + nature symbols
- Read inward or outward

**Cerulean Flow**
- Flowing, artistic
- 32 characters
- Connected letters

## RELIGION & MYTHOLOGY
*(Briefly, as this could be its own document)*

**Polytheism (Most Cultures):**
- Multiple gods, nature spirits
- Differs by culture but some overlap
- Syncretism common in trade cities

**Ancestor Worship:**
- Dwarves: Clan founders deified
- Sylvans: Ancestors in trees
- Debrican: Hearth spirits

**Animism:**
- Sylvans: Everything has spirit
- Vorthic: Desert spirits, djinn-like

**Dragon Mythology:**
- Vorthic: Dragons are ancestors
- Dwarves: Dragons hoard gold (enemies)
- Universal: Dragons exist/existed (evidence ambiguous)
