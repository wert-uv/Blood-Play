# SOUTHERN ISLAND - RUINS OF SOLIVIRUM

**Position:** South of the main western island, isolated in southern waters
**Terrain Type:** Rocky island with degraded structures
**Description:** A medium-sized island significantly separated from the main archipelago. The "ruins" designation suggests a major settlement once existed here but has been abandoned. The island's isolation would have made it strategically valuable but also vulnerable.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- The island has an irregular, almost star-shaped coastline
- Extensive stone ruins concentrated on the eastern side
- What appears to be a former large palace or temple complex
- Harbor facilities now in disrepair
- The western side of the island is less developed
- Some areas of terraced agriculture now overgrown
- A central hill or small mountain (200-300 meters elevation)
- Possible former defensive walls around the entire settlement
- Beach areas on the southern and northern coasts
- Vegetation has reclaimed much of the cleared land
- Several smaller ruined structures scattered across the island
