## NORTHWESTERN CONTINENT - BOREAS

**Position:** Western-central portion of map, south of The Girdle's western section
**Geographic Type:** Major continental landmass
**Approximate Size:** 1,500 km north-south by 1,000 km east-west (rough estimate)

### Overall Description
The largest single landmass in the western portion of the visible map. The name "Boreas" (often associated with north or north wind in various mythologies) suggests this is a northern continent with cooler climates. The landmass shows significant topographic variation.

### Northern Boreas - Highland Region

**Position:** Northern third of the continent
**Terrain:** Extensive mountain system
**Description:** A substantial mountain range dominates northern Boreas, running roughly east-west with some north-south trending branches. These mountains appear comparable in scale to the central range of Aridonia.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- Primary mountain chain extends 400-500 km east-west
- Multiple peaks visible even at this continental scale
- Mountain range creates a climate divide for the continent
- Northern slopes likely receive heavy precipitation
- Southern slopes likely in rain shadow
- Several valleys cut through the range (4-5 visible at this scale)
- Eastern extension of the range curves southward
- A significant gap in the mountains in the center may be a major pass
- Possible glaciation in the highest peaks

### Central Boreas - Interior Lowlands

**Position:** Central region of Boreas
**Terrain:** Lowland plains with possible river systems
**Description:** A broad lowland region south of the northern mountains, appearing flatter and potentially more hospitable. The lighter coloring suggests different vegetation or land use compared to surrounding areas.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- Several major river systems appear to flow southward (3-4 major rivers)
- Possible large lake or inland sea in the east-central area
- Terrain appears to transition from temperate in the north to possibly semi-arid in parts of the south
- Evidence of dendritic drainage patterns
- A large basin or depression in the central-eastern area
- Possible agricultural potential in river valleys

### Southern Boreas - Coastal Regions

**Position:** Southern portion of the continent
**Terrain:** Varied coastline with peninsulas and bays
**Description:** The southern margin of Boreas features a complex coastline with multiple peninsulas, bays, and what appears to be a large gulf or inland sea indentation on the eastern side.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- A major peninsula extends southward in the southwest (200 km long)
- Deep bay or gulf cuts into the eastern side of the continent
- Several smaller peninsulas along the southern coast (4-5 visible)
- Island clusters offshore to the south and east (approximately 15-20 small islands)
- Varying coastal terrain from cliffs to lowlands
- Possible delta regions where major rivers meet the coast
- A chain of barrier islands along parts of the southern coast

### Western Boreas - Coastal Highlands

**Position:** Western edge of the continent
**Terrain:** Rugged coastline with elevated terrain
**Description:** The western coast appears more mountainous or at least more elevated than the interior, with a generally straighter coastline suggesting fault-line origin.

**Notable Unnamed Features:**
- Coastal mountains or highlands parallel to the shore
- Steep coastal slopes limiting harbor sites
- A few deep fjord-like indentations (3-4 visible)
- Small offshore islands (5-10) along the coast
- Possible volcanic features in the northwest
- Limited coastal plains - mountains meet the sea
