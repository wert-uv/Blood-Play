### The Setting: The Azure Archipelago

- **Visuals:** Beautiful, vibrant floating islands connected by vines and shaky bridges. Waterfalls flow off the edges into infinite white clouds below.
    
- **The Vibe:** It feels like paradise. The weather is perfect. The fruits are sweet. This stark beauty is necessary to contrast the ugliness of the truth later (the _Midsommar_ effect).
    
- **The Inhabitants:**
    
    - **The Orangs:** Tall, gentle, furred humanoids with high intelligence and a tribal, peaceful culture.
        
    - **The Fauna:** Dinosaurs (Raptors, Triceratops, etc.), but they are oddly docile or skittish. 

---

### Phase 1: The Hook & The Lie

To get players invested, you must first make them love the people here, then introduce the tragedy.

**The Lie:** The players arrive at the main Orang village. They are welcomed. They notice there are **only females** (Orangs and Dinosaurs).

- **Lore Drop:** The Orangs explain their "Curse." They say that when a female reaches "The Blooming" (maturity), she is summoned by the **Sky Spirit** to the **Sanctuary** (a fortress on the highest island).
    
- **The Tragedy:** They believe the Sky Spirit blesses them with a child, but the mother’s life force is consumed in the process. They view this as a holy sacrifice. They never see the mother again, only the baby that is "returned" to the village steps.
    

**The Emotional Anchor (Crucial):** Introduce an NPC named **Elara**.

- She is a young, spirited Orang who helps the party navigate the islands.
    
- She is distinct—maybe she loves music or wants to learn swordplay from Lucien.
    
- **The Kicker:** She is days away from "The Blooming." She is terrified of dying, but willing to do it for her people. **Make the players promise to protect her.**
    

---

### Phase 2: The Cracks in the Facade

As the party explores (perhaps hunting a rogue T-Rex or finding parts for a ship), drop clues that something is wrong.

1. **The Dinosaur Encounter:** The party fights a T-Rex. Halfway through, it vomits up something indigestible—a metal chain collar. Wild animals don't wear collars.
    
2. **The "Male" Bones:** In a ravine (the trash chute of the Sanctuary), they find piles of small bones. A Medicine Check reveals they are _infant_ bones. If they use magic/science, they realize these are all _male_ infants.
    
3. **The Doctor's Envoy:** The Doctor (The Doppel) comes down from the Sanctuary. He appears as a beautiful, glowing celestial humanoid (a fake form). He is charismatic, kind, and "heals" a party member. He comes to collect Elara early.
    

---

### Phase 3: The Borrasca Reveal

The party should refuse to let Elara go, or decide to escort her. They infiltrate the Sanctuary (The Highest Island).

**The Atmosphere Shift:** As they climb, the blue sky turns grey. The air smells of antiseptic and rot. The sound of "The Borrasca" (a mechanical grinding or a low, magical hum) gets louder.

**The Dungeon:** The Sanctuary isn't a temple. It’s a **factory**.

- **Room 1:** The Nursery. Where he kills the male offspring and cleans the female ones to look "divine."
    
- **Room 2:** The "Stable." This is the horror reveal.
    
    - Rows of cages.
        
    - Here, they find the "Dead" mothers. They aren't dead. They are chained, emaciated, and kept alive via magic solely for breeding.
        
    - The Dinosaurs are here too, strapped down.
        
    - **The Shock:** If they look closely at the eyes of a "Dinosaur" in a cage, it looks back with terrifying intelligence. (The Doctor transforms into the male version of the species to breed).
        

---

### Phase 4: The Boss Fight & The Dilemma

The players confront **The Doctor** in his laboratory.

**The Villain:** The Doctor (Doppel) is arrogant. He doesn't see himself as evil; he sees himself as the **Savior**.

- _Monologue:_ "Without me, they cease to exist. I am their husband, their father, their god. I am the only reason their bloodline endures. You call this slavery? I call it survival."
    

**Combat Mechanics (The Mutated Changeling):** The Doctor shouldn't just fight as a humanoid. He has absorbed the DNA of everything he has bred with.

- **Phase 1:** **The T-Rex Hybrid.** He morphs his arms into T-Rex jaws. High physical damage.
    
- **Phase 2:** **The Orang Alpha.** He gains agility and uses the environment, swinging from pipes.
    
- **Phase 3:** **The Mirror.** He turns into **Lucien** or **Issac**. He uses their exact stats and abilities against them for 2 rounds.
    

---

### The Climax: The Impossible Choice

Once the Doctor is defeated (brought to 1 HP or restrained), he laughs.

**The Ultimatum:** _"Kill me, and you commit genocide. I am the last male. I am the only one who can carry the seed. Strike me down, and these 'precious' Orangs will age and die, leaving nothing behind but dust. I am the bottleneck of evolution."_

The players have to choose:

1. **Kill Him:** The immediate horror ends. The mothers are freed. But the Orangs and Dinosaurs are doomed to a slow extinction (unless the players can find a magical solution later, e.g., Val researching cloning or finding a way to reverse the gender imbalance).
    
2. **Spare Him:** The species survives, but the torture continues (perhaps they try to force him to be "humane," but can you trust a monster?).
    
3. **The Third Path (Player Creativity):** Maybe Issac’s Blood Crystal can change biology? Maybe Val can synthesize the Doctor's shapeshifting ability into a potion?
    

**The Ending of the Layer:** Regardless of the choice, the "Paradise" facade is broken. The sky turns grey. The players leave the layer likely feeling heavy, having saved the individuals (Elara and the mothers) but perhaps doomed the future.

### Summary for your notes:

- **Atmosphere:** Studio Ghibli visuals hiding a _Silent Hill_ reality.
    
- **NPC:** **Elara** (Innocent Orang girl).
    
- **Villain:** **The Doctor** (Mutated Changeling/Doppelganger).
    
- **Key Item:** **The Doctor’s Journal** (Details his breeding experiments and the extinction timeline).
    
- **The twist:** The "Dead" mothers are the "Livestock."
