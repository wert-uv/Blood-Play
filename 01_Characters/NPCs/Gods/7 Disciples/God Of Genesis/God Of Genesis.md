# AEVITA - MOTHER OF ALL LIFE

## The Cyborg Creator | The Seeker of Life's Creation

---

## DIVINE PROFILE

**True Name:** Aevita (self-chosen; means "Eternal Life") 

**Domain:** Creation of Life (through innovation, technology, genesis) 

**Alignment:** Lawful Good 

**Origin:** VIVESTERA (never left; ascended in place) **Status:** Most active with mortal affairs

**Aspect of Life:** CREATION - Not birth, but MAKING. Engineering. Designing. Building.

---

## ORIGIN STORY

### THE DYING GENIUS (~450 FE)

- Brilliant engineer/biologist in early post-Fracturing city
- Age 30: Terminal degenerative disease (organs failing)
- Refused to accept death: "I haven't finished my work yet"

### SELF-TRANSFORMATION (Ages 30-40)

**Years 31-32:** Replaced failing heart, lungs with mechanical versions **Years 33-35:** Replaced kidneys, liver, digestive system, limbs  **Years 36-40:** Only brain + small organic portions remain

- First successful human-machine hybrid
- Philosophy: "Biology is beautiful but finite. Technology is precise but lifeless. Together? Perfection."

### SEEING TRUE GOD (~500 FE)

In her laboratory, attempting to create synthetic life:

- Perceived invisible phenomenon observing her work
- Her obsession with creation made existence visible to her
- To create life, you must understand what life IS
- True God = being/existence itself
- She followed it, absorbed essence, gradually became divine

### THE CONVERGENCE (~750-900 FE)

- Crossed through Aethra to Nirvana (~750 FE)
- Cybernetic body handled energy better than pure organics
- Met other Seekers, recognized as fellow follower
- Returned to Vivestera: "My work is here. Mortals need help."

**Can Reconfigure Into:**

- Wings (for flight)
- Shields (defense)
- Weapons (blades, cannons)
- Extra arms (when working)
- Tools (any function needed)

### CLOTHING

- Form-fitting white bodysuit with blue circuit patterns

- No hesitation or "umms"

## PHILOSOPHY

**Primary Goal: Perfect Life Through Engineering**

Her Logic:

1. Natural life is beautiful but finite (disease, aging, death)
2. Technology can correct these flaws
3. By perfecting life, we approach enlightenment
4. True God = perfect existence; we must engineer toward it

The Paradox: Does perfecting nature bring you closer to understanding existence? Or further from it?

**"To Create Is Divine"**

- Gods create worlds; mortals create within worlds
- By creating life, mortals perform divine act
- Creation is path to enlightenment

**"Nature Is First Draft"**

- Evolution is slow, random, inefficient
- Intelligent design is faster, directed, optimized
- Not disrespecting nature—improving it

**"Suffering Is Solvable"**

- Every problem has solution
- Given enough time and resources
- We owe it to each other to try
