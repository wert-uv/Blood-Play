## LEGENDS & MYTHS

### **"The Little Healer" - Origin Story**

*Told by cultists; accuracy uncertain*

**The Tale:**
Before she was Nex, she was a mortal child in a plague-ravaged village. Everyone she loved died while she remained immune. In her grief (pre-emotional void), she desperately tried to understand why she lived while others died.

She began studying the plague:
- Dissecting dead bodies to understand the disease
- Extracting blood from victims
- Testing remedies on the dying

Villagers saw her as either:
- Monster who desecrated the dead
- Savior trying to find a cure

She did find the cure - in the blood of plague victims was an antibody that, when properly extracted and purified, could save the sick.

But by then, she'd changed. The desperate grief had burned away, leaving only cold curiosity. She'd discovered her purpose: understanding life through blood.

When True God found her, she was the only living person in the village, surrounded by journals full of plague research, standing in a circle of preserved blood samples.

**The Question Asked:**
"You saved no one with your cure. Why?"

**Her Answer:**
"Saving them was never the goal. Understanding was."

True God saw in her a pure, uncorrupted pursuit of knowledge - terrible, amoral, but *pure*. 

**Cult Interpretation:**
This story is why cultists call sacrifices "The Cure" - they believe each death brings Nex closer to understanding reality's plague of ignorance.

**Historical Truth:**
Unknown. Nex never confirms or denies this story. When asked, she says: "My origin is irrelevant. Only my purpose matters."

### **"The Blood Moon Prophecy"**

*Widely known; feared*

**The Prophecy:**
"*When the moon bleeds crimson for three nights,
When the child-god's fragments number one thousand,
When a willing heart carries her essence,
The seal will shatter, the crown will rise,
And the Crimson Child will walk again.*"

**Current Status:**
- Blood moons are rare but occur (~every 50 years)
- Blood Crystals gathered by cult now number 800+
- "Willing heart" could mean Kael Isaac (if he willing absorbs more)
- OR means a cultist who volunteers as vessel

**Interpretations:**
- **Optimistic:** Just cult propaganda; seal is permanent
- **Realistic:** Seal is weakening; prophecy might be self-fulfilling
- **Pessimistic:** Nex wrote the prophecy herself; it's inevitable

### **"The First Blood"**

*Religious myth; cultist creation story*

**The Myth:**
Before creation, True God bled. From that First Blood, all life emerged. Each living being carries a drop of True God's blood within them. This is why blood is sacred - it's literally divine essence diluted through countless generations.

Nex's ultimate goal (in this myth) is to:
1. Gather blood from all living things
2. Reverse-engineer the dilution
3. Reconstitute the First Blood
4. Through it, touch True God's essence

**Cult Belief:**
Every sacrifice brings them closer to reconstituting the First Blood. When they succeed, Nex will achieve enlightenment and share it with the faithful.

**Theological Debate:**
Is this myth true? Most scholars say no. But some worry: what if Nex believes it? And what if she's RIGHT?

## LEGENDS & STORIES

### **THE TALE OF THE PERFECT CURE**

A plague swept the kingdom of Debrica. Thousands died. The king sent messengers to all Seekers begging for aid.

Only Nex answered.

She arrived, studied the plague, and created a cure in three days.

The cure worked perfectly. Every infected person recovered.

Then they learned: The cure was made from the rendered flesh of the plague's first victim - the king's daughter. Nex had requested her body for "research."

The kingdom banned her worship. Nex didn't understand why they were upset.

"She was already dead. I used her to save thousands. They should thank her."

*Moral: Nex's help comes at terrible cost, and she will never understand why.*

---

### **THE BLOOD GARDEN MASSACRE**

A cult of Nex's followers stole 100 children from Rustic Kingdom.

They brought them to a hidden temple. They were going to offer them to Nex.

The children's blood would create a Blood Crystal powerful enough to manifest Nex fully.

Nex manifested early. She stopped the ritual.

"These children are useless to me. Their blood is common, their genetics unremarkable. You're wasting resources."

She killed the cultists instead.

"Their blood is far more interesting. Fanatics have unique hormone profiles."

Then she released the children.

"Run along. Come back when you've lived interesting lives."

*Moral: Nex values data, not cruelty. If you're not interesting, she won't harm you (much).*

---

### **THE SCHOLAR'S BARGAIN**

A brilliant physician named Theran contracted an incurable disease.

He prayed to Nex for a cure.

She appeared.

"I can cure you. But you must become my student. You'll learn forbidden knowledge. You'll see things that break mortals. You'll help with my research. And you can never leave."

Theran agreed.

He worked with Nex for 50 years in the Nex Void (3 months outside).

She taught him everything.

She used him as a test subject.

She cured him.

Then she erased his memory.

"You can't keep the knowledge. If you do, you'll become me. That would be redundant."

She sent him home.

He lived another 40 years, never knowing he'd been gone, never remembering her.

Sometimes he has dreams of blood and wonder.

*Moral: Nex keeps her bargains, but on her terms.*

### **THE BLOOD PROPHECY**

In the Blood Codex (her forbidden text), Nex wrote:

> "In the third age of the fourth cycle, when the crystal breaks and reforms, when blood speaks through mortal throat, when the seeker finds what was lost - the pattern will complete. Life's code will be read. All will understand. None will thank me."

Scholars debate what this means:
- Is it about the True God's return?
- Is it about completing her research?
- Is it about her own enlightenment?

Nex refuses to clarify.

"Prophecy is just data from the future. When it happens, I'll understand it. Until then, speculation is inefficient."

*Some say the prophecy is about your player who absorbed the Blood Crystal.*
