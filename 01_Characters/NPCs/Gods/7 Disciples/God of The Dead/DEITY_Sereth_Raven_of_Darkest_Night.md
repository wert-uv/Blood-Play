- Skull visible through torn skin
- Left eye: Empty socket, glowing blue-white flame
- Skeletal jaw, exposed teeth
- Bone protrudes through skin
- Left wing: Bone and tendons, no feathers

**Build:** Slender but strong (living side), skeletal (dead side)

- Vertical line from crown to feet

- Sharp, perfect divide

**Wings:**

- Asymmetrical flight pattern (lurching, haunting)
- Feathers constantly fall from right wing
- Bones occasionally crack on left wing

**The Raven Motif:**

- Perch on her shoulders

- Spirit ravens favor left side

- Some are actually dead souls

**Clothing:**
- Black robes with silver trim
- Tattered on left side (decay)
- Pristine on right side (renewal)

**The Aura:**
Being near Sereth:
- Living side: Comforting, peaceful

- Both simultaneously: Deeply disturbing
- Mortals struggle to process her existence
- Some faint, some weep, some laugh
- She's used to it

## BACKSTORY - THE TWICE-BORN

### **CELESTIAL LIFE (3,400 Years Ago)**

**Original Nature:**
Sereth was born in Nirvana - the layer where mortal souls evolve into celestial beings.

**What is a Nirvana Celestial?**
- Amalgamation of countless mortal souls
- Bathed in energy seeping from Zenith
- Radiate divine power
- Eventually become something beyond mortal comprehension
- Beautiful, powerful, eternal

**Her Form:**
She was a guardian angel:
- Protected souls in transition
- Guided newly dead to Nirvana
- Helped them integrate
- Fought things that preyed on souls
- Perfect, beautiful, pure

**Her Nature:**
Unlike other celestials:
- She remembered being mortal (most forget)
- She felt compassion (most transcend emotion)
- She cared about individuals (most see collective)
- She questioned the cycle (most accept)

**The Question:**
Where do souls come from before birth?
Where do they go after Nirvana?

She asked the True God during its pilgrimage through Nirvana.

---

### **THE MEETING**

**The Conversation:**
> Sereth: "Great One, I must know: what lies beyond Nirvana? Where do we go when we've evolved past this layer?"
> 
> True God: "Why does it matter?"
> 
> Sereth: "Because I guide souls here. I tell them they're going somewhere better. But I don't know if that's true."
> 
> True God: "Do you doubt?"
> 
> Sereth: "I doubt everything. That's why I need to know."
> 
> True God: "Then seek. But know: to truly understand death, you must die. And to understand undeath, you must refuse to die."
> 
> Sereth: "I'm already dead. I was mortal once. I died and became this."
> 
> True God: "No. You transcended. You never truly died. Would you like to?"

She said yes.

---

### **THE FALL**

**What the True God Did:**

The True God reached out and tore her left wing off.

**The Pain:**
- Beyond physical
- Spiritual agony
- Divine essence bleeding
- Falling from Nirvana to Vivestera
- Plummeting through layers
- Burning

**The Death:**
She died on impact in Vivestera.

Her body shattered. Her divine essence scattered. Her consciousness fragmented.

She was dead.

Completely.

Finally.

**But:**

In the moment of death, she understood:
- What mortality meant
- What ending felt like
- The terror, the peace, the release
- The cycle's purpose
- Why death was necessary

And she refused to accept it.

---

### **THE RESURRECTION**

**The Choice:**

In the space between death and rebirth:
- She saw the cycle clearly
- Understood she should let go
- Move on to the next stage
- Accept transformation

But she thought of all the souls she'd guided. Who would guide them if she was gone?

**The Refusal:**
She clawed her way back.

Not resurrection - that implies help.
Not reincarnation - that implies acceptance.

**Undeath** - refusing to move on.

She forced her divine essence back into her shattered body. But:
- The body was dead (couldn't heal)
- Her wing was gone (torn by the True God)
- Half her flesh had rotted (time passed during death)
- Her power was fractured (some essence lost)

**The Result:**
She became the first undead celestial:
- Divine being that died
- Refused to stay dead
- Caught between life and death
- Forever split down the middle

**The True God's Response:**
It appeared to her as she stood, half-dead, broken.

> True God: "Now you understand."
> 
> Sereth: "I do. Death is necessary. The cycle must continue."
> 
> True God: "Then why did you break it?"
> 
> Sereth: "Because souls need a guide. Someone who understands both sides."
> 
> True God: "Or because you were afraid."
> 
> Sereth: "...Yes. That too."
> 
> True God: "Then seek. But carry this burden: you will guide others through what you yourself refused. You will tell them to accept what you rejected. You will be forever the hypocrite god."

And it left.

**The Transformation:**
She was now:
- Half-living, half-dead
- Half-angel, half-corpse
- Power over death (from dying)
- Power over undeath (from refusing)
- Eternal guilt (from her hypocrisy)

She became the God of the Dead.

Not because she accepts death.

Because she refuses it.

### **THE DIVERGENCE - THE FIGHT WITH NEX**

**2,500 Years Ago:**

At the great meeting of Seekers, Nex proposed:
"We should dissect the True God to understand Enlightenment."

Sereth's reaction was immediate and visceral.

**Why It Triggered Her:**

1. **Sacred Desecration:**
   - The True God gave her death and understanding
   - Nex wanted to tear that apart
   - It was blasphemy

2. **Personal Trauma:**
   - Nex studies life by destroying it
   - Sereth died (traumatic)
   - Nex treating death as data horrified her

3. **Philosophical Opposition:**
   - Sereth believes in respecting cycles
   - Nex believes in breaking them for knowledge
   - Fundamental incompatibility

4. **The Deeper Reason:**
   - Nex doesn't fear death
   - Sereth fears it terribly (died once)
   - Nex's casualness about death is offensive
   - Reminds Sereth of her own cowardice

**The Fight:**

Sereth attacked first.

Bad decision.

**Powers Clashed:**
- Sereth: Death magic, soul manipulation, decay
- Nex: Blood magic, life manipulation, biology

**The Problem:**
- Nex can't truly die (immortal)
- Sereth is already dead (undead)
- Neither can kill the other

**What Almost Happened:**
Nex nearly won by:
- Stopping Sereth's blood flow
- Decaying her living half further
- Studying her undeath mid-fight
- "Fascinating! Your cellular structure is contradictory!"

Sereth nearly killed herself by:
- Overusing death magic
- Accelerating her own decay
- Rage-induced loss of control
- "I'LL DRAG YOU INTO DEATH WITH ME!"

**The Intervention:**
Other Seekers stopped them.

Vorgath physically restrained Sereth.
Imperion mentally suppressed Nex.

**The Aftermath:**
- Sereth's living half more damaged
- Nex completely unbothered
- "That was excellent data."
- Sereth: "I WILL END YOU!"
- Nex: "You can't even end yourself."

That last comment made Sereth weep.

Because it was true.

**The Sealing Vote:**
When vote came to seal Nex:
- Sereth voted yes (obviously)
- Actually lobbied for complete destruction
- "She'll never stop. She's too dangerous."
- Nex: "You just don't want me to study you."
- Sereth: "YES. OBVIOUSLY. BECAUSE YOU'RE HORRIFYING."

Nex was sealed.

But Sereth knows: one day, she'll return.

And Sereth will have to fight her again.

## LEGENDS & STORIES

### **THE TALE OF THE SOLDIER'S CHOICE**

A soldier named Marcus died in battle.

His body was taken by necromancers.

They raised him as undead servant.

Sereth found him 50 years later, still serving.

She offered him choice:
"I can destroy you. Free your soul. End this existence."

Marcus refused.

"My family is still alive. I watch over them. I can't leave yet."

Sereth understood.

She did what she swore she'd never do.

She blessed his undeath.

"Then serve as guardian. Not slave. Guard your family. When they're safe, come find me. I'll give you peace then."

Marcus guards his family still.

Ten generations later.

Waiting to be ready to die.

*Moral: Sereth breaks her rules for love.*

---

### **THE RAVEN'S MESSAGE**

A young mother lay dying of plague.

Her children were too young to understand.

She begged Sereth: "Help them understand. They'll be so confused."

Sereth appeared as a raven.

Spoke to the children in their dreams.

Explained death gently.

"Your mother is becoming something beautiful. Like caterpillar to butterfly. She'll always love you. She'll be waiting when your time comes. But now, you must be brave."

The children woke understanding.

They were sad but not afraid.

When their mother died, they released a raven.

"Fly to mama. Tell her we love her."

The raven flew to Nirvana.

Carried their love.

Their mother felt it.

*Moral: Sereth cares for the living left behind.*

---

### **THE DEATH OF DEATH**

A necromancer named Valthus raised an army of undead.

Planned to conquer the world.

"I'll make everyone immortal! No one needs to die!"

Sereth appeared before him.

"You cannot end death. Death is transformation. You're not granting immortality. You're denying evolution."

He attacked her.

His undead army attacked her.

She stood still.

And spoke one word.

"**Rest.**"

Every undead in his army collapsed.

Souls freed.

Finally at peace.

Valthus stood alone.

"You... you killed them all?"

"No. I freed them. Death isn't ending. You made them prisoners. I released them."

She killed Valthus.

Gently.

He died peacefully despite his crimes.

"Everyone deserves a peaceful death. Even those who deny it to others."

*Moral: Sereth's mercy extends to everyone.*

---
