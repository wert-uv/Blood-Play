# SERETH - THE RAVEN OF DARKEST NIGHT
## The Undead Celestial | The Seeker of Life's Rebirth

---

## DIVINE PROFILE

**True Name:** Sereth (celestial name; her mortal name lost to time)
**Titles:**
- The Raven of Darkest Night (primary)
- God of the Dead (common mortal understanding)
- The Half-Death (derogatory, by Nex)
- The Torn Angel (poetic)
- She-Who-Guards-The-Crossing (Umbra spirits)
- The Merciful End (by those seeking death)
- The Eternal Mourner (philosophical texts)

**Domain:** The Rebirth of Life (through death, transformation, and renewal)
**Alignment:** Neutral Good (leans Lawful in execution)
**Layer of Origin:** Nirvana (was celestial; fell but didn't descend to lower layers)
**Current Location:** Umbra (the boundary between death and life)
**Status:** Active; divided between Nirvana and Umbra

**Aspect of Life:** REBIRTH
- Where others represent life's creation or pursuit, Sereth embodies the cycle of ending and beginning again. Death is not destruction but transformation.

## APPEARANCE

### **The Duality Form**

Sereth's appearance is a stark, tragic duality - beautiful and horrifying split down the center:

**Left Side (Living):**
- **Skin:** Pale but luminous, with faint silver glow
- **Eye:** Brilliant silver-white, filled with compassion
- **Hair:** Long, flowing black (like raven feathers)
- **Wing:** Perfect white feathered wing (celestial)
- **Expression:** Serene, gentle, understanding

**Right Side (Dead):**
- **Skin:** Rotting flesh over exposed bone; patches of desiccation
- **Eye:** Empty socket with faint blue spirit-fire
- **Hair:** Same black, but brittle and falling out
- **Wing:** Skeletal wing structure with remnant feathers
- **Expression:** Fixed in rictus, cannot change

**The Torn Wing:**
- **Left Wing:** Pristine white feathers, each perfect
- **Right Wing:** Skeleton with scattered black feathers
- **The Tear:** Where wing meets body, a jagged wound that never heals
- **Significance:** The point where Nex's magic corrupted her

**Height:** 6'2" (188 cm) - taller than average mortal
**Build:** Slender, graceful on living side; emaciated on dead side
**Weight:** Varies (living side has mass; dead side weighs almost nothing)

---

### **The Details**

**Clothing:**
- **Style:** Flowing robes split between white and black
- **Living Side (White):** Pristine, embroidered with silver threads forming patterns of phoenixes, butterflies, seeds sprouting
- **Dead Side (Black):** Tattered, holes showing bone beneath, embroidered with silver patterns of skulls, ravens, autumn leaves
- **Junction:** The split between colors is a gradient of gray

**Additional Features:**
- **Crown:** Circlet of silver thorns intertwined with dead roses (half withered, half blooming)
- **Hands:** Left hand warm and alive; right hand cold skeletal fingers
- **Feet:** Left foot solid; right foot sometimes incorporeal (phases slightly)
- **Shadow:** Cast shadow shows her "complete" form (both wings whole, both sides living)

**Voice:**
- Dual-toned: Living side speaks with warm, melodious voice
- Dead side speaks with hollow, echoing voice
- Both speak simultaneously (creates eerie harmony)
- Those who hear her can choose which voice to focus on

**Aura:**
- Living side radiates warmth, comfort, hope
- Dead side radiates cold, finality, peace
- Combined effect: Bittersweet acceptance
- Dying people find her presence soothing; living find her unsettling

## PERSONALITY

### **Core Traits**

#### **Compassionate Yet Distant**
Sereth deeply cares about all living things, but her nature keeps her apart:

**Manifestations:**
- Genuinely grieves every death (experiences it as personal loss)
- Provides comfort to dying and bereaved
- Never causes unnecessary death
- Yet can't truly connect with the living (she's half-dead herself)

**The Tragedy:**
She loves life but can never fully live again. She guides souls to rebirth but cannot be reborn herself. She is stuck in transition, eternally.

---

#### **Patient and Understanding**
Unlike many deities, Sereth doesn't judge:

**Why:**
- Has seen death from every angle
- Understands that all paths lead to her eventually
- Knows that harsh lives create desperate actions
- Believes death reveals truth of a person

**Application:**
- Accepts the worst sinners without condemnation (that's Gehenna's job)
- Treats murder victims and murderers with equal grace
- Tells people "You tried. That's enough."
- Sees potential for rebirth in everyone

---

#### **Melancholic Optimism**
Sereth carries deep sadness yet maintains hope:

**The Sadness:**
- Mourns her lost wholeness
- Grieves every soul that passes through Umbra
- Carries weight of billions of deaths
- Lonely despite constant company of spirits

**The Hope:**
- Believes in cycles - endings enable beginnings
- Trusts that death is not final (rebirth exists)
- Finds beauty in transformation
- Sees potential in even the darkest souls

**Quote:**
"I am forever dying, yet I live. I am broken, yet I serve. I grieve eternally, yet I hope endlessly. This is the paradox of death - it ends nothing, only transforms."

---

#### **Protective of Souls**
Sereth's primary drive: ensuring safe passage for the dead:

**Why It Matters:**
- Souls in Umbra are vulnerable to demons, soul-eaters, necromancers
- Many spirits get lost in the transition
- Some refuse to move on (ghosts)
- Others are forcibly trapped

**Her Actions:**
- Guards Umbra personally
- Battles demons who hunt souls
- Retrieves lost spirits
- Negotiates with necromancers (sometimes violently)
- Comforts those not ready to move on

---

#### **Hatred (For Nex Only)**
Sereth's only true hatred - cold, eternal, absolute:

**Why Different from Other Conflicts:**
- This isn't philosophical disagreement
- This is personal betrayal
- Nex didn't just hurt her - Nex BROKE her
- Sereth blames Nex for her eternal half-death

**Manifestation:**
- Normally calm Sereth becomes wrathful when Nex is mentioned
- Her rotting side becomes more animated (rage energizes death)
- Speaks in harsh, overlapping tones
- Eyes burn with cold blue fire

**The Vow:**
"When the Blood God breaks her seal - and she will - I will be there. Not to imprison her again. To END her. She fears nothing, feels nothing, understands nothing. It's time she learned DEATH."

---

### **Motivations & Philosophy**

#### **Primary Goal: Master Death and Rebirth**
Sereth seeks enlightenment by understanding the transition:

**Her Logic:**
1. Life and death are not opposites but phases
2. The transition contains the secret of existence
3. By mastering the in-between state, she approaches enlightenment
4. Helping souls transition helps her understand the process

**The Irony:**
She IS the in-between state (half-alive, half-dead), but doesn't understand her own nature. She seeks externally what she embodies internally.

---

#### **Secondary Goal: Heal Herself**
Despite claims to accept her state, Sereth wants wholeness:

**What She's Tried:**
- Prayer to True God (no answer)
- Healing magic (doesn't work on divine corruption)
- Time (2,000+ years; no improvement)
- Studying life and death magic (hoping for insight)
- Accepting the wound (doesn't work either)

**The Truth:**
Only Nex could heal her (has the biological knowledge). Nex won't (doesn't see the point). This fuels Sereth's hatred.

---

#### **Philosophy of Death**

**"Death is the Kindest Gift"**
Sereth believes:
- Mortality gives life meaning
- Death is release from suffering
- The cycle of rebirth allows growth
- Immortals are trapped, not blessed

**"All Deserve Passage"**
No matter what someone did in life:
- They deserve peaceful death
- Their soul deserves safe guidance
- Judgment happens elsewhere (Gehenna or Nirvana)
- Her job is compassion, not condemnation

**"Transformation is Sacred"**
Death isn't ending:
- Caterpillar to butterfly
- Autumn to spring
- Mortal to celestial
- Ending to beginning

**"I Am the Proof"**
Her own half-death state is theological argument:
- Neither alive nor dead, yet both
- Neither whole nor broken, yet both
- This proves the transition is real state, not just moment
- Death isn't instant switch - it's process

## LEGENDS & MYTHS

### **"The First Death" - Creation Myth**

*Told by Death Priests; accuracy unknown*

**The Tale:**
In the beginning, nothing died. Life was created by Aevita, but couldn't end. The world filled with life until there was no room. Life suffered, crushed together, unable to grow.

True God saw this and wept. From those tears, the first soul was separated from body. The body stopped, the soul ascended. Death was born.

But death was harsh and terrifying. Souls were torn violently from bodies and cast into void. True God saw this was wrong.

From Nirvana, a celestial volunteered: "Let me guide them. Let me make death gentle." This was Sereth, before she was broken.

She became the first Death Goddess, guiding souls with grace and compassion. True God blessed her: "You shall be the bridge between life and death, never fully one or the other."

And so she was blessed... until Nex corrupted her, and the blessing became curse.

**Theological Debate:**
- Did Sereth exist before being celestial?
- Was she always meant to be broken (to understand transition)?
- Did True God foresee Nex's betrayal?

### **"The Broken Wing"**

*Actual history; documented*

See Nex file for full account. Sereth's version:

**"The Betrayal of Blood"**

*I trusted her. That was my first mistake.*

*The blood-child came to Nirvana as all souls do, screaming and fighting transformation. Most souls resist at first - it's natural. But she was different. She didn't fear change. She wanted to study it.*

*She asked to observe transformations. I allowed it. She asked to measure energies. I permitted it. She asked to witness the moment of metamorphosis. I agreed.*

*Then she asked: "What if we stopped the process? Just for a moment. To see what happens."*

*I should have said no. But I was curious too. We were both seekers of knowledge, I thought. We could learn together.*

*The soul I was transforming... she stopped it. Mid-metamorphosis. The soul didn't ascend. Didn't return. It ENDED. True death. Impossible death.*

*I attacked her. She defended herself with blood magic I'd never seen. She said "How fascinating! You're trying to hurt me! Let me study your reaction--"*

*And she did. She studied how celestial wings tear. How divine flesh corrupts. How life and death can exist in same body. She turned me into her experiment.*

*I should have died. Would have died. But Teacher arrived.*

*The blood-child became Teacher's disciple. I became Teacher's lesson in accepting brokenness.*

*I learned the lesson. But I never forgave her.*

### **"The Raven's Promise"**

*Common folktale; basis in truth*

**The Story:**
A mother's child was dying of plague. She prayed to all gods. Only Sereth answered.

The goddess appeared in her mourner form, holding the dying child.

"Please," the mother begged, "save my child!"

Sereth shook her head. "Her time has come. The disease has spread too far. Even I cannot reverse this."

"Then what good are you?" the mother cried.

Sereth was quiet for long moment. Then: "I can do three things. I can make her death painless. I can ensure her soul travels safely to Nirvana. I can return her to you in another life."

"Another life?" the mother asked.

"Reincarnation. She'll be born again, nearby. You'll recognize her eyes. She won't remember you, but you'll remember her. Is this enough?"

The mother wept, but nodded.

The child died painlessly in Sereth's arms. Her soul ascended peacefully.

Three years later, a child was born in the same village with the same eyes. The mother knew, and loved her again.

**The Promise:**
Sereth keeps promises to grieving parents. She brings their children back if they ask. She never asks for payment, prayer, or worship.

This is why parents pray to her most of all gods.

### **"The Undead Uprising"**

*Historical event - The Necromancer Wars*

800 years ago, a powerful necromancer raised an army of undead, conquering several kingdoms. Other Seekers were occupied with Imperion situation.

Sereth acted alone.

She walked into the necromancer's throne room. Undead soldiers surrounded her.

She spoke one word: "ENOUGH."

Every undead in the empire collapsed. Their souls were freed, returning to Umbra. The necromancer's power broke instantly.

The necromancer attacked her. She didn't defend. She simply waited.

When his spell struck her torn wing, she caught it with her skeletal hand. The death magic flowed into her dead side, absorbed harmlessly.

"You misunderstand death," she told him. "You see it as power to control. I am death. You cannot control me."

She touched him. He died instantly. His soul? She guided it to Gehenna personally.

**The Lesson:**
Don't fuck with the Death Goddess. She will simply wait for you to die (and you will), then she'll have eternity to judge you.

- If she seems angry - step back (rare but dangerous)

## DIVINE PARADOXES

Sereth embodies contradictions that make her theologically fascinating:

**Living Dead:**
- Cannot fully live, cannot fully die
- Exists in permanent transition
- Proof that life and death can coexist

**Compassionate Reaper:**
- Death is her domain but she mourns each one
- Takes life but grieves taking it
- Necessary evil that hates being necessary

**Broken Healer:**
- Cannot heal herself but heals others
- Guides souls to wholeness while remaining fractured
- Teaches acceptance she hasn't fully achieved

**Merciful Judge:**
- Doesn't judge souls but determines their destination
- Offers comfort but delivers finality
- Kind executioner

These paradoxes are why she seeks enlightenment - she IS the thing she tries to understand. Like Nex pursuing life through death, Sereth pursues understanding of transition BY BEING transition.
