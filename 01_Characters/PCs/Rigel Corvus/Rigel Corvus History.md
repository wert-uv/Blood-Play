*Rigel Corvus: An Eldritch Knight*
---
"Run, Rigel. You have to live."

 His boots were caked with mud, and the dagger weighted heavily on his belt. Blood dripped off his clothes. He hadn't had a moment to clean them. He was out of breath, yet he kept running on the unmarked terrain of the forest. His breath grew haggard, cold inhales stinging his lungs.

Why won’t you stop! They need my help. Goddamit! turn away and go save them.

He heard running water ahead, past the forest clearing lay a river, moonlight shining bright above. The glow on the water shimmered with stillness and peace, a sharp contrast to what was happening in his village, as if nature mocked him. 

Coward..Coward..ran away, showing your back to your people. I am such a COWARD!!

Rigel stared at the dried blood on his cloak, a silent chill creeping down his spine as his father’s final moments replayed in his mind. Suddenly, he felt a dampness spreading across the fabric at his chest. He looked down—two droplets fell to the forest floor. For a heartbeat, he thought it must be the rain. He glanced up, but found only the undisturbed, moonlit expanse. Frowning, he pressed trembling fingers to his cheeks and felt the unmistakable wetness of tears. Only then did he realise he’d been crying.

---

Rigel Corvus was from a small town of Astral Elderwood, a home of wizards and sorcerers. Despite being a hardworking and kind-hearted child, Rigel was derided by his father, for he lacked the talent of being a great wizard. Unlike his elder brother, Alrakis Corvus was the pride of their father and the town. The strongest and bravest among everyone in the town. 

Despite knowing his shortcomings, Rigel worked hard on himself. Unfortunately, all his efforts and grit amounted to average at best. His father never acknowledged his struggle. For him, talent was above all, though he never belittled him for it; he simply never cared for him. For all his attention was on Alrakis, he made no stone unturned to make his elder son become the greatest there is.

After the tragedy of Astral Elderwood, Rigel stumbled into an orphanage in a nearby town. He was one of the oldest there. He watched when younger children arrived, most of them orphans from war or sickness. Some of them were dropped off as infants, with no memory of parents at all. 

With a similar past, the kind-hearted Rigel became more of a caretaker than the actual caretakers were. The children looked up to Rigel and loved him for his ability to make them laugh and his silly, goofy nature. Most of all, they loved him for his bedtime stories. He would make them up every night, telling stories about stars, his town, and his brother's adventures.

---

“Brother, what are you making?”
“Hamburg steak. It’s your birthday, isn’t it?.. Don’t tell the rest of the family”

A teardrop traced down Rigel’s cheek as he slowly woke up. The cold wind pressed his face, telling him it was the start of early winter. The remnants of a dream, a memory of the past, still lingered at the edges of his mind. To the side of his bed was a pile of gifts given by the children and caretakers of the orphanage. Today was his birthday. From the strangers and caretakers who mourned his loss, Rigel had learned about the tradition of his town: to give Hamburg steak to acknowledge people on their birthdays. 

They probably just didn’t care about me that much; there’s no place for the weak.I guess my brother might’ve been different. But when the village was attacked, I abandoned even my brother and fled! No surprise they never celebrated my birthday. A failure who ran from everything. A hopeless Coward.

The knock at the door ended his thoughts. Rigel got out of his bed and went to thank everyone for the gifts.
---

A violent clang split the air as steel clashed against steel. 

Rigel’s breath came in heavy bursts, his arms trembling from the strain of lifting his longsword to parry against the relentless blows. The fury of attacks came from every direction, some direct while others were masked with fients so precise that it broke Rigel’s stance.

He faltered.

Rigel went spiralling on the ground after receiving the back hilt of the sword. The familiar taste of iron in his mouth. 

“You have good focus Rigel, but that extreme focus narrows your vision and lefts you open. Build your structure and hold your ground. With the right foundation, even the strongest attacks can be dealt with ease. Now get up. We’re finished”
“Yes, Master”

Garrick Rickman is a seasoned warrior, retired from his duties. Rigel encountered him not long after living in the orphanage, when Garrick stumbled upon him during his visiting of the town. After a few lucky encounters, Garrick recognised Rigel’s physical prowess, though he lacked the magical talent compared to Astral Elderwood’s citizen who were known for their magical abilities. Garrick took an interest in the young boy. Over time, he taught Rigel the art of combat, swords, daggers, battleaxes, and the fundamentals of a swordsman. 

Garrick, as veteran and skilled as he is in sword fighting, when it comes to wizardry, he is nothing more than a coughing baby. But he knows that talent can be surpassed by sheer hard work. So, he has helped in any way necessary for his arcane studies.
